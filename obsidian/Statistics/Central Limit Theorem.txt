sampling distribution

- probability distribution of sample statistic that describes the variability of random sampling of the same size from the same population
---
standard error

- standard deviation of sampling distribution
---
mean standard error formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\sigma_\mu = \frac{\sigma}{\sqrt n} \\
\sigma = \text{standard deviation} \\
n = \text{sample size}
\end{align*}
$$
---
proportion standard error formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\sigma = \sqrt{\frac{p(1-p)}{n}} \\
p = \text{proportion} \\
n = \text{sample size}
\end{align*}
$$
---
sum standard error formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\sigma = \sigma\sqrt n \\
\sigma = \text{standard deviation} \\
n = \text{sample size}
\end{align*}
$$
---
law of large numbers

- sample mean converges population mean as sample size gets large ($n\ge 30$) 
---
formula of large numbers

$$
\begin{align*}
n \rightarrow \infty \Rightarrow \bar X \rightarrow \mu \\
\end{align*}
$$
---
central limit theorem

- as sample size gets large the sampling distribution approaches normal distribution regardless of the population distribution
---
central limit formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\bar X \sim N(\mu, \frac{\sigma}{\sqrt n}) \\
\mu = \text{mean} \\
\sigma = \text{standard deviation} \\
n = \text{sample size}
\end{align*}
$$
---
estimator

- statistic capable of estimating the value of parameter
---
biased estimator

- mean of statistic not equal parameter
---
unbiased estimator

- mean of statistic equal parameter
---
z score

- number of standard deviations from the mean
---
mean z score formula

$$
\begin{align*}
z = \frac{\bar X - \mu}{\sigma/\sqrt n} \\
\bar X = \text{sample mean} \\
\mu = \text{population mean} \\
\sigma = \text{population standard deviation} \\
n = \text{sample size}
\end{align*}
$$
---
proportion z score formula

$$
\begin{align*}
z = \frac{\hat p - p}{\sqrt{\frac{p(1-p)}{n}}} \\
\hat p = \text{sample proportion} \\
p = \text{population proportion} \\
n = \text{sample size}
\end{align*}
$$
---
sum z score formula

$$
\begin{align*}
z = \frac{\sum X - \mu n}{\sigma \sqrt n} \\
\mu = \text{mean} \\
n = \text{sample size} \\
\sigma = \text{standard deviation}
\end{align*}
$$
