random variable

- variable whose values equal the outcomes of random process
---
random variable formula

$$
\begin{align*}
X: \Omega \rightarrow \mathbb{R} \\
X = \text{random variable} \\
\Omega = \text{sample space} \\
\mathbb{R} = \text{real number}
\end{align*}
$$
---
discrete random variable

- finite number of countable values
---
discrete probability distribution function

- probability as function of discrete random variable values
---
discrete PDF probability formula

$$
\begin{align*}
0 \le P(x) \le 1 \\
\sum P(x) = 1
\end{align*}
$$
---
discrete PDF mean formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\mu = \sum P(x)x
\end{align*}
$$
---
discrete PDF standard deviation formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\sigma = \sqrt{\sum(x - \mu)^2P(x)}
\end{align*}
$$
---
expected value

- average value of random variable or the mean of PDF  
---
expected value formula

$$
\begin{align*}
E(X) = \sum_i P(x_i)x_i
\end{align*}
$$
---
binomial probability distribution

- probability as function of the number of successes
---
binomial PDF characteristics

- binary random variable
- definite number of trials 
- sampling with replacement
---
binomial PDF probability formula

$$
\begin{align*}
P(x) = C^n_xp^xq^{n-x} \\
n = \text{number of trials} \\
x = \text{number of successes across n trials} \\
p = \text{probability of success} \\
q = \text{probability of failure}
\end{align*}
$$
---
binomial PDF mean formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\mu = np \\
n = \text{number of trials} \\
p = \text{probability of success}
\end{align*}
$$
---
binomial PDF standard deviation formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\sigma = \sqrt {npq} \\
n = \text{number of successes} \\
p = \text{probability of success} \\
q = \text{probability of failure}
\end{align*}
$$
---
memoryless property

- likelihood event will occur independent prior occurrences
---
memoryless formula

$$
\begin{align*}
P(X > x + k | X > x) = P(X > k)
\end{align*}
$$
---
geometric probability distribution

- probability as function of 1st success
---
geometric PDF characteristics

- binary random variable
- memoryless probability
- indefinite number of trials
- sampling with replacement
- 1st random variable equal number of trials until 1st success
- 2nd random variable equal number of failures before 1st success
---
geometric PDF probability formula

$$
\begin{align*}
P(x) = (1-p)^{x-1}p \\
p = \text{probability of success} \\
x = \text{number of failures before 1st success}
\end{align*}
$$
---
geometric PDF mean formula 

$$
\begin{align*}
\mu = \frac{1}{p} \\
p = \text{probability of success}
\end{align*}
$$
---
1st geometric PDF standard deviation formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\sigma = \sqrt{(\frac{1}{p})(\frac{1}{p}-1)} \\
p = \text{probability of success}
\end{align*}
$$
---
2nd geometric PDF standard deviation formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\sigma = \sqrt{(\frac{1}{p}-1)} \\
p = \text{probability of success}
\end{align*}
$$
---
hypergeometric probability distribution function

- probability as function of the number of items drawn from the group of interest
---
hypergeometric PDF characteristics

- definite number of trials 
- sampling without replacement
---
hypergeometric PDF probability formula

$$
\begin{align*}
P(x) = \frac{C^K_x C^{N-K}_{n-x}}{C^N_n} \\
K = \text{interest group size} \\
x = \text{number of interest items drawn} \\
N = \text{population size} \\
n = \text{number of items drawn}
\end{align*}
$$
---
hypergeometric PDF mean formula 

$$
\begin{align*}
\mu = \frac{nK}{N} \\
N = \text{population size} \\
K = \text{interest group size} \\
n = \text{number of items drawn}
\end{align*}
$$
---
hypergeometric PDF standard deviation formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\sigma = \sqrt{(\frac{nK}{N})(1 - \frac{K}{N})(\frac{N-n}{N-1})} \\
n = \text{number of items drawn} \\
K = \text{interest group size} \\
N = \text{population size}
\end{align*}
$$
---
poisson probability distribution function

- probability as function of events within interval
---
poisson PDF characteristics

- approximate binomial PDF for small probability of success and large number of trials
- constant average number of events
- independent events 
---
poisson PDF probability formula

$$
\begin{align*}
P(x) = \frac{e^{-\mu}\mu^x}{x!} \\
\mu = \text{average number of events}
\end{align*}
$$
---
poisson PDF mean formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\mu = \mu \\
\mu = \text{average number of events}
\end{align*}
$$
---
poisson PDF standard deviation formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\sigma = \sqrt \mu \\
\mu = \text{average number of events}
\end{align*}
$$
