estimator

- statistic capable of estimating the value of parameter
---
point estimate

- single best guess for the value of parameter
---
confidence interval

- range of guesses for the value of parameter
---
mean confidence interval formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\bar x \pm z^*(\frac{\sigma}{\sqrt n}) \\
\bar x \pm t^*(\frac{s}{\sqrt n}) \\
\bar x = \text{mean} \\
z^* = \text{critical z value} \\
t^* = \text{critical t value} \\
\sigma = \text{population standard deviation} \\
s = \text{sample standard deviation} \\
n = \text{sample size}
\end{align*}
$$
---
proportion confidence interval formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\hat p \pm z^* \sqrt {\frac{\hat p(1-\hat p)}{n}} \\
\hat p = \text{proportion} \\
z^* = \text{critical value} \\
n = \text{sample size}
\end{align*}
$$
---
standard deviation confidence interval formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\sqrt{\frac{(n-1)s^2}{\chi^2_{\alpha/2}}} \leq \sigma \leq \sqrt{\frac{(n-1)s^2}{\chi^2_{1-\alpha/2}}} \\
n = \text{sample size} \\
s = \text{sample standard deviation} \\
\chi = \text{critical value}
\end{align*}
$$
---
correct confidence interval

- 95% confidence that the value of parameter is inside the confidence interval
---
incorrect confidence interval

- 95% chance that the value of parameter is outside the critical region  
- 95% of statistic is between lower boundary and upper boundary
---
confidence level

- probability of the parameter being inside the confidence interval
![[Statistics/Images/confidence level.png|300]]
---
confidence level formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\text{1-tail CL} = 1-2\alpha \\
\text{2-tail CL} = 1-\alpha \\
\alpha = \text{significance level}
\end{align*}
$$
---
critical z value

- normal distribution significance level
![[Statistics/Images/critical z value.png]]
---
critical z value formula

$$
\begin{align*}
z^*_{two} = \text{invNorm}(1-\frac{\alpha}{2}, 0, 1) \\
z^*_{left} = \text{invNorm}(\alpha, 0, 1) \\
z^*_{right} = \text{invNorm}(1-\alpha, 0, 1) \\
\alpha = \text{significance level}
\end{align*}
$$
---
margin of error

- maximum likely amount of error between statistic and parameter
---
margin of error formula

$$
\begin{align*}
E = \text{critical value} \times \text{standard error}
\end{align*}
$$
---
calculate point estimate via confidence interval

- upper confidence interval addition with lower confidence interval  
- sum division with two
---
calculate point estimate via margin of error

- upper confidence interval subtraction with margin of error
---
calculate margin of error via confidence interval

- upper confidence interval subtraction with lower confidence interval  
- difference division with two
---
calculate margin of error via point estimate

- upper confidence interval subtraction with point estimate
---
sample size

- number of observations as function of margin of error
---
mean sample size formula

$$
\begin{align*}
n = (\frac{z^*\sigma}{E})^2 \\
z^* = \text{critical value} \\
\sigma = \text{standard deviation} \\
E = \text{margin of error}
\end{align*}
$$
---
proportion sample size formula

$$
\begin{align*}
n = (\frac{z^*\sqrt {\hat p(1-\hat p)}}{E})^2 \\
z^* = \text{critical value} \\
\hat p = \text{proportion} \\
E = \text{margin of error}
\end{align*}
$$
---
sample proportion

- point estimate of population proportion
---
proportion confidence interval requirements

- random sampling
- trial independence
- at least 5 successes and 5 failures
---
construct proportion confidence interval  

- find critical value
- calculate margin of error
- $\hat p - E < p < \hat p + E$ 
---
sample mean

- point estimate of population mean
---
mean confidence interval requirements

- random sampling
- trial independence
- known population standard deviation
- normal population distribution or $n\ge 30$ 
![[Statistics/Images/mean confidence interval requirements.png|300]]
---
construct mean confidence interval

- find critical value
- calculate margin of error
- $\bar x - E < \mu < \bar x + E$ 
---
t distribution

- normal distribution except fatter tails for extra uncertainty when estimating the population standard deviation and dependence on degrees of freedom
---
degrees of freedom

- number of sample data that can vary without changing estimate
---
degrees of freedom formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\text{df} = n-1 \\
n = \text{sample size}
\end{align*}
$$
---
critical t value

- t distribution significance level
![[Statistics/Images/critical t value.png|400]]
---
critical t value formula

$$
\begin{align*}
t^*_1 = \text{invT}(1-\alpha, \text{df}) \\
t^*_2 = \text{invT}(1-\frac{\alpha}{2},\text{df}) \\
\alpha = \text{significance level} \\
\text{df} = \text{degrees of freedom}
\end{align*}
$$
---
sample standard deviation

- point estimate of population standard deviation
---
standard deviation confidence interval requirements

- random sampling
- trial independence
- normal population distribution
---
construct variance confidence interval

- find both critical values
- $\sqrt{\frac{(n-1)s^2}{\chi^2_{\alpha/2}}} \leq \sigma \leq \sqrt{\frac{(n-1)s^2}{\chi^2_{1-\alpha/2}}}$ 
---
chi square distribution

- probability distribution of all possible sample variances from same size samples of the same population
---
critical chi value

- chi square distribution significance level
![[Statistics/Images/critical chi value.png|300]]
---
calculate critical chi value

- right tail area equal half of significance level
- left tail area equal 1 subtraction with half of significance level
- chi square table
