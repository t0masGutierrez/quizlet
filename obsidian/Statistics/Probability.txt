sample space

- set of all possible outcomes
---
event

- subset of sample space
---
probability

- likelihood event will occur
---
law of large numbers

- as random experiment repeats the relative frequency of event tends to approach true probability
---
frequentist probability

- relative frequency
---
frequentist probability formula

$$
\begin{align*}
P(A) = \lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{|A|}{n} \\
\end{align*}
$$
---
classical probability

- equally likely outcomes
---
classical probability formula

$$
\begin{align*}
P(A) = \frac{\text{number of favorable outcomes}}{\text{total number of outcomes}} \\
0 \le P(A) \le 1
\end{align*}
$$
---
complimentary probability

- likelihood event will not occur
---
complimentary probability formula

$$
\begin{align*}
P(A') = 1 - P(A)
\end{align*}
$$
---
conditional probability

- likelihood event A will occur given event B already occur
---
conditional probability formula

$$
\begin{align*}
P(A | B) = \frac{P(A \cap B)}{P(B)}
\end{align*}
$$
---
independent event

- event B outcome not dependent upon event A outcome
- with replacement
---
independent multiplication rule

- likelihood event A and event B will occur given event B independent event A
---
independent multiplication formula

$$
\begin{align*}
P(A \cap B) = P(A) P(B)
\end{align*}
$$
---
dependent event

- event B outcome dependent upon event A outcome
- without replacement
---
dependent multiplication rule

- likelihood event A and event B will occur given event B dependent event A
---
dependent multiplication formula

$$
\begin{align*}
P(A \cap B) = P(A) P(B | A)
\end{align*}
$$
---
disjoint event

- two events cannot occur at same time
---
disjoint addition rule

- likelihood event A or event B will occur given event B mutually exclusive event A
---
disjoint addition formula

$$
\begin{align*}
P(A \cup B) = P(A) + P(B)
\end{align*}
$$
---
joint event

- two events can occur at same time
---
joint addition rule

- likelihood event A or event B will occur given event B mutually inclusive event A
---
joint addition formula

$$
\begin{align*}
P(A \cup B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A \cap B)
\end{align*}
$$

- method of updating probability of hypothesis based on new evidence
---
bayes formula

$$
\begin{align*}
P(A | B) = P(B | A) P(A) + P(B)
\end{align*}
$$

combinatorics

- branch of mathematics that counts arrangements of objects
---
permutation

- number of ways to arrange objects with order
---
permutation formula

$$
\begin{align*}
P^n_k = \frac{n!}{(n-k)!} \\
n = \text{number of objects} \\
k = \text{number of arrangements}
\end{align*}
$$
---
calculate permutation

- n factorial for k arrangements
---
combination

- number of ways to arrange objects without order
---
combination formula

$$
\begin{align*}
C^n_k = \frac{n!}{k!(n-k)!} \\
n = \text{number of objects} \\
k = \text{number of arrangements}
\end{align*}
$$
---
calculate combination

- n factorial for k arrangements  
- product division with k factorial because null repeatability
---
combinatorial probability

- equally likely outcomes
---
combinatorial probability formula

$$
\begin{align*}
P(A) = \frac{\text{number of favorable combinations}}{\text{number of possible combinations}}
\end{align*}
$$
---
odds for

- likelihood event will occur
---
odds for formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\frac{P(A)}{P'(A)}
\end{align*}
$$
