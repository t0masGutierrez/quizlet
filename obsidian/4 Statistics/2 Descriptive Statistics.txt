class

- divide continuous range of data into equal, consecutive, and distinct interval
---
class formula

$$
\begin{align*}
k = 1 + \lceil \frac{\log n}{\log 2}\rceil \\
w = \frac{\max(x) - \min(x)}{k} \\
k = \text{number of classes} \\
w = \text{class width} \\
n = \text{sample size}
\end{align*}
$$
---
class midpoint

- mean difference between upper class endpoint and lower class endpoint
---
class midpoint formula

$$
\begin{align*}
m_i = \frac{E_i + E_{i+1}}{2} \\
E = \text{class endpoint}
\end{align*}
$$
---
histogram mean

- average histogram
---
histogram mean formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\mu = \frac{\sum_{i=1}^n f_im_i}{\sum_{i=1}^n f_i} \\
n = \text{sample size} \\
f = \text{frequency} \\
m = \text{class midpoint} 
\end{align*}
$$
---
histogram standard deviation

- average difference between histogram and mean
---
histogram standard deviation formula

$$
\begin{align*}
s = \sqrt{\frac{\sum_{i=1}^nf_im_i^2}{n} - \mu ^2} \\
n = \text{sample size} \\
f = \text{frequency} \\
m = \text{class midpoint} \\
\bar x = \text{mean}
\end{align*}
$$

---
outlier

- significant deviation from the mean
---
lower outlier formula

$$
\begin{align*}
x = \text{Q1} - 1.5 \times \text{IQR} \\
\text{Q1} = \text{1st quartile} \\
\text{IQR} = \text{interquartile range}
\end{align*}
$$
---
upper outlier formula

$$
\begin{align*}
x = \text{Q3} + 1.5 \times \text{IQR} \\
\text{Q3} = \text{3rd quartile} \\
\text{IQR} = \text{interquartile range}
\end{align*}
$$

measure of position

- location of data

---
z score

- number of standard deviations from mean
---
z score formula

$$
\begin{align*}
z = \frac{x - \mu}{\sigma} \\
x = \text{datum} \\
\bar x = \text{mean} \\
s = \text{standard deviation}
\end{align*}
$$

percentile

- convert percentile into data
---
percentile formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\text{sort}(x) \rightarrow i = \frac{k}{100}(n+1) \\
x = \text{data} \\
i = \text{index} \\
k = \text{percentile} \\
n = \text{sample size}
\end{align*}
$$
---
inverse percentile

- convert data into percentile
---
inverse percentile formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\text{sort}(x) \rightarrow k = 100(\frac{|x_j < x_i| + 0.5|x_j = x_i|}{n}) \\
x = \text{data} \\
k = \text{percentile} \\
x_j = \text{nontarget data} \\
x_i = \text{target data} \\
n = \text{sample size}
\end{align*}
$$
