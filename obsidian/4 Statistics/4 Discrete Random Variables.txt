random variable

- variable whose values equal the outcomes of random process
---
random variable formula

$$
\begin{align*}
X: \Omega \rightarrow \mathbb{R} \\
X = \text{random variable} \\
\Omega = \text{sample space} 
\end{align*}
$$
---
discrete random variable

- finite number of countable values
---
discrete random variable formula

$$
\begin{align*}
(\{0, 1, 2, 3, \dots, n\} \sim X) \land (\mathbb N \sim X) \\
\end{align*}
$$
---
discrete probability distribution function

- probability as function of discrete random variable values
---
discrete PDF probability formula

$$
\begin{align*}
0 \le P(X = x) \le 1 \\
P(X) = \sum_i P(X = x_i) = 1 \\
P(a \le X \le b) = \sum_{i=a}^b P(X = x_i)
\end{align*}
$$
---
discrete PDF mean formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\mu = \sum_i P(X=x_i)x_i
\end{align*}
$$
---
discrete PDF standard deviation formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\sigma = \sqrt{\sum_i(x_i - \mu)^2P(x_i)}
\end{align*}
$$
---
bernoulli probability distribution

- probability as function of the number of successes from single trial
---
bernoulli PDF assumptions

- single trial
- two outcomes
---
bernoulli PDF probability formula

$$
\begin{align*}
P(X = k) = pq^{1-k} \\
n = \text{number of trials} \\
k = \text{number of successes} \\
p = \text{probability of success} \\
q = \text{probability of failure} 
\end{align*}
$$
---
bernoulli PDF mean formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\mu = p \\
p = \text{probability of success}
\end{align*}
$$
---
bernoulli PDF standard deviation formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\sigma = \sqrt {pq} \\
p = \text{probability of success} \\
q = \text{probability of failure}
\end{align*}
$$
---
binomial probability distribution

- probability as function of the number of successes
---
binomial PDF assumptions

- two outcomes
- fixed number of trials
- constant probability of success
- independent trials
---
binomial PDF probability formula

$$
\begin{align*}
P(X = k) = C(n, k)p^kq^{n-k} \\
n = \text{number of trials} \\
k = \text{number of successes} \\
p = \text{probability of success} \\
q = \text{probability of failure} 
\end{align*}
$$
---
binomial PDF mean formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\mu = np \\
n = \text{number of trials} \\
p = \text{probability of success}
\end{align*}
$$
---
binomial PDF standard deviation formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\sigma = \sqrt {npq} \\
n = \text{number of successes} \\
p = \text{probability of success} \\
q = \text{probability of failure}
\end{align*}
$$
---
geometric probability distribution

- probability as function of the number of trials until 1st success
---
geometric PDF assumptions

- two outcomes
- random number of trials
- constant probability of success
- independent trials
---
geometric PDF probability formula

$$
\begin{align*}
P(X = k) = pq^{k-1} \\
p = \text{probability of success} \\
q = \text{probability of failure} \\
k = \text{number of trials until 1st success}
\end{align*}
$$
---
geometric PDF mean formula 

$$
\begin{align*}
\mu = \frac{1}{p} \\
p = \text{probability of success}
\end{align*}
$$
---
geometric PDF standard deviation formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\sigma = \frac{\sqrt {1 - p}}{p} \\
p = \text{probability of success}
\end{align*}
$$
---
hypergeometric probability distribution function

- probability as function of the number of items drawn from the group of interest
---
hypergeometric PDF assumptions

- two outcomes
- fixed number of trials
- variable probability of success
- dependent trials
---
hypergeometric PDF probability formula

$$
\begin{align*}
P(X = k) = \frac{C(K, k)C(N-K, n-k)}{C(N,n)} \\
K = \text{interest group size} \\
k = \text{number of interest items drawn} \\
N = \text{population size} \\
n = \text{number of items drawn}
\end{align*}
$$
---
hypergeometric PDF mean formula 

$$
\begin{align*}
\mu = \frac{nK}{N} \\
n = \text{number of items drawn} \\
K = \text{interest group size} \\
N = \text{population size} 
\end{align*}
$$
---
hypergeometric PDF standard deviation formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\sigma = \sqrt{(\frac{nK}{N})(1 - \frac{K}{N})(\frac{N-n}{N-1})} \\
n = \text{number of items drawn} \\
K = \text{interest group size} \\
N = \text{population size}
\end{align*}
$$
---
poisson probability distribution function

- probability as function of events within interval
- approximate binomial PDF with small probability of success and large number of trials
---
poisson PDF assumptions

- fixed interval
- constant average number of events per interval
- independent events 
- disjoint events
---
poisson PDF probability formula

$$
\begin{align*}
P(X = k) = \frac{e^{-\lambda}\lambda^k}{k!} \\
\lambda = \text{average number of events per interval} \\
k = \text{number of events}
\end{align*}
$$
---
poisson PDF mean formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\mu = \lambda \\
\lambda = \text{average number of events per interval}
\end{align*}
$$
---
poisson PDF standard deviation formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\sigma = \sqrt \lambda \\
\mu = \text{average number of events per interval}
\end{align*}
$$
