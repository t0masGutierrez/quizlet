proper subspace

- subset of vector space whom itself equal vector space with the same operations
---
proper subspace formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\mathcal W < \mathcal V \leftrightarrow (\mathcal W \subset \mathcal V) \land (\mathcal W \ne \{\vec 0\}) \land (\mathcal W \in \mathcal V) \\
\mathcal W = \text{subspace} \\
\mathcal V = \text{vector space}
\end{align*}
$$
---
improper subspace

- subset of vector space whom itself equal vector space with the same operations and subset possibly equal vector space or equal zero vector
---
improper subspace formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\mathcal W \le \mathcal V \leftrightarrow (\mathcal W \subseteq \mathcal V) \lor (\mathcal W = \{\vec 0\}) \land (\mathcal W \in \mathcal V) \\
\mathcal W = \text{subspace} \\
\mathcal V = \text{vector space}
\end{align*}
$$
---
trivial subspace

- subset of vector space equal zero vector
---
trivial subspace formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\mathcal W = \{\vec 0\} \\
\mathcal W = \text{subspace} \\
\mathcal V = \text{vector space} \\
\vec 0 = \text{zero vector}
\end{align*}
$$
---
subspace property

- subspace must satisfy closure of vector space and existence of zero vector
---
subspace property formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\mathcal W \le \mathcal V \leftrightarrow (c\vec w \in \mathcal V) \land (\vec w_1 + \vec w_2 \in \mathcal V) \land (\exists \vec 0)\\
\mathcal W \ne \emptyset \\
\mathcal W = \text{subspace} \\
\mathcal V = \text{vector space} \\
\vec 0 = \text{zero vector}
\end{align*}

$$---
example subspace

- lower triangular matrix
- upper triangular matrix
- diagonal matrix
- symmetric matrix
- skew symmetric matrix
---
example subspace formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\mathcal L_n \le\mathcal M_n \\
\mathcal U_n \le\mathcal M_n \\
\mathcal D_n \le\mathcal M_n \\
\mathcal A^T \le\mathcal M_n \\
\end{align*}
$$

example nonsubspace

- nonsingular matrix
- singular matrix
- rref square matrix
---
example nonsubspace formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\{A| \det(A) \ne 0\} \not\le\mathcal M_n \\
\{A| \det(A) = 0\} \not\le\mathcal M_n \\
\{\text{rref}(A)\} \not\le\mathcal M_n
\end{align*}
$$

intersection property

- intersection of subspaces of vector space equal member of subspace
---
intersection property formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\mathcal W_1, \mathcal W_2 \in \mathcal W \rightarrow (\mathcal W_1 \cap \mathcal W_2) \in \mathcal W \\
\mathcal W = \text{subspace}
\end{align*}
$$
---
zero vector property

- zero vector equal member of every subspace
---
zero vector property formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\mathcal W \le\mathcal V \leftrightarrow \vec 0 \in \mathcal W \\
\mathcal W = \text{subspace} \\
\mathcal V = \text{vector space} \\
\vec 0 = \text{zero vector}
\end{align*}
$$
---
linear combination property

- linear combination of the vectors of subspace equal member of subspace
---
linear combination property formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\vec w_1, \dots , \vec w_n \in \mathcal W \rightarrow c_1\vec w_1 + \dots + c_n\vec w_n \in \mathcal W \\
\vec w = \text{vector} \\
\mathcal W = \text{subspace} \\
c = \text{scalar}
\end{align*}
$$

two element subspace

---
three element subspace

- linear combination form space through the origin

eigenspace property

- eigenspace equal subspace of $n$ dimensional real numbers
---
eigenspace property formula

$$
\begin{align*}
E_{\lambda} \le \mathbb R^n \\
A \in \mathcal M_n \\
A = \text{square matrix} \\
\lambda = \text{eigenvalue} \\
E_{\lambda} = \text{eigenspace} 
\end{align*}
$$
