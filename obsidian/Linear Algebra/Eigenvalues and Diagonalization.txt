eigen

- characteristic of the transformation
---
eigenvalue

- scalar quantity that describes the magnitude of scalar multiplication with the corresponding eigenvector during transformation
---
eigenvalue formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\lambda \leftrightarrow \exists\vec x, A\vec x = \lambda \vec x \\
\vec x \ne 0 \\
\lambda = \text{eigenvalue} \\
\vec x = \text{eigenvector} \\
A = \text{square matrix}
\end{align*}
$$
---
eigenvector

- n-vector with variable magnitude but constant direction
---
eigenvector formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\vec x \leftrightarrow \exists\vec x, A\vec x = \lambda \vec x \\
\vec x \ne 0 \\
\lambda = \text{eigenvalue} \\
\vec x = \text{eigenvector} \\
A = \text{square matrix}
\end{align*}
$$
---
eigenspace

- set of all eigenvectors for matrix associated with eigenvalue
---
eigenspace formula

$$
\begin{align*}
E_{\lambda} = \{\vec x | A \vec x = \lambda \vec x \} \\
\vec x = \text{eigenvector} \\
A = \text{square matrix} \\
\lambda = \text{eigenvalue}
\end{align*}
$$
---
number of eigenvectors

- scalar multiplication with eigenvector equal eigenvector
---
number of eigenvectors formula

$$
\begin{align*}
c \in \mathbb R \rightarrow c\vec x \in E_{\lambda} \\
c = \text{scalar} \\
\vec x = \text{eigenvector} \\
E_{\lambda} = \text{eigenspace}
\end{align*}
$$
---
homogeneous system of linear equations

- constant matrix equal zero matrix
---
homogeneous system of linear equations formula

$$
\begin{align*}
(\lambda I - A)\vec x = 0 \\
\lambda = \text{eigenvalue} \\
I = \text{identity matrix} \\
A = \text{square matrix} 
\end{align*}
$$
---
singular eigenvalue property

- eigenvalue if and only if determinant of coefficient matrix equal zero
---
singular eigenvalue property formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\lambda \leftrightarrow |\lambda I - A| = 0 \\
\lambda = \text{eigenvalue} \\
I = \text{identity matrix} \\
A = \text{square matrix}
\end{align*}
$$
---
homogeneous solution set

- the eigenvectors for the corresponding eigenvalue are the nontrivial solutions of the homogeneous system of linear equations
- the eigenspace for the corresponding eigenvectors is the complete solution set of homogeneous system of linear equations
---
characteristic polynomial

- polynomial whose real roots equal the eigenvalues of matrix
---
characteristic polynomial formula

$$
\begin{align*}
p_A(x) = |xI - A| = 0 \\
x = \text{dummy variable} \\
I = \text{identity matrix} \\
A = \text{square matrix}
\end{align*}
$$
---
diagonalization

- compute characteristic polynomial
- test diagonalizability
- substitute eigenvalues into homogeneous system of linear equations
- form the reduced row echelon of the system
- fundamental solutions of homogeneous system of linear equations equal fundamental eigenvectors
- form eigenmatrix such that column vectors equal eigenvectors
- test diagonalizability
- compute inverse eigenmatrix
- matrix multiplication equal diagonal matrix
- all entries along main diagonal of diagonal matrix equal eigenvalue 
---
diagonalization formula

$$
\begin{align*}
D = P^{-1}AP \leftrightarrow A = PDP^{-1} \\
|P| \ne 0 \\
P = \text{eigenmatrix} \\
A = \text{square matrix} \\
D = \text{diagonal matrix} \\
P^{-1} = \text{inverse eigenmatrix} 
\end{align*}
$$
---
similarity

- similar matrices represent the same transformation but different coordinate system
- similar matrices have the same size
- similar matrices are similar with themselves
- similar matrices are commutative
- similar matrices are transitive
- similar matrices with identity matrix equal identity matrix
- similar matrices have equal determinant
- similar matrices have equal characteristic polynomial
---
similarity formula

$$
\begin{align*}
A \sim D \leftrightarrow \exists P, D = P^{-1}AP \\
|P| \ne 0 \\
A = \text{square matrix} \\
D = \text{diagonal matrix} \\
P = \text{eigenmatrix} \\
P^{-1} = \text{inverse eigenmatrix}
\end{align*}
$$
---
geometric multiplicity

- dimension of eigenspace or number of independent variables
---
geometric multiplicity formula

$$
\begin{align*}
E_{\lambda} = \{\sum_i^k c_i \vec x_i | c \in \mathbb R\} \\
i = \text{eigenvector index} \\
k = \text{geometric multiplicity} \\
c = \text{scalar} \\
\vec x = \text{eigenvector}
\end{align*}
$$
---
diagonalizability 

- sum of geometric multiplicity equal size of square matrix if and only if diagonalizable
---
diagonalizability formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\sum k = n \leftrightarrow \exists P \\
|P| \ne 0 \\
k = \text{geometric multiplicity} \\
n = \text{number of rows} \\
n = \text{number of columns} \\
P = \text{eigenmatrix}
\end{align*}
$$
---
algebraic multiplicity

- power of eigenvalue
---
algebraic multiplicity formula

$$
\begin{align*}
p_A(x) = (x - \lambda)^{k_0}(x - \lambda)^{k_1}\dots(x - \lambda)^{k_n} \\
\lambda = \text{eigenvalue} \\
k = \text{algebraic multiplicity}
\end{align*}
$$
---
diagonalizability 

- sum of algebraic multiplicity equal size of square matrix if and only if diagonalizable
---
diagonalizability formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\sum k = n \leftrightarrow \exists P \\
|P| \ne 0 \\
k = \text{algebraic multiplicity} \\
n = \text{number of rows} \\
n = \text{number of columns} \\
P = \text{eigenmatrix}
\end{align*}
$$
---
matrix exponentiation

- square matrix exponentiation similar diagonal matrix exponentiation
---
matrix exponentiation formula

$$
\begin{align*}
A^k = PD^kP^{-1} \\
P = \text{eigenmatrix} \\
D = \text{diagonal matrix} \\
k = \text{positive integer} \\
P^{-1} = \text{inverse eigenmatrix}
\end{align*}
$$
