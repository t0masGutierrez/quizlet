linear dependence

- zero vector expressible as nontrivial linear combination of the vectors of subset of vector space
---
linear dependence formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\text{Rank}(S) < n \rightarrow \vec x \ne \vec 0 \\
S = [\vec v_1, \dots, \vec v_n] \subseteq \mathcal V \\
S = \text{subset} \\
n = \text{number of columns} \\
\vec x = \text{solution} \\
\mathcal V = \text{vector space}
\end{align*}
$$
---
linear independence

- zero vector not expressible as nontrivial linear combination of the vectors of subset of vector space
---
linear independence formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\text{Rank}(S) = n \rightarrow \vec x = \vec 0 \\
S = [\vec v_1, \dots, \vec v_n] \subseteq \mathcal V \\
S = \text{subset} \\
n = \text{number of columns} \\
\vec x = \text{solution} \\
\mathcal V = \text{vector space}
\end{align*}
$$
---
empty set

- linearly independent set with zero elements
---
empty set formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\emptyset = \{\}
\end{align*}
$$
---
example linear independence

- standard unit vector
- polynomial
- standard unit matrix
---
example linear independence formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\vec e \\
\vec p \\
\psi_{ij}
\end{align*}
$$
---
single element linear independence

- subset with single nonzero vector equal linearly independent subset of vector space
---
single element linear independence formula

$$
\begin{align*}
(c_1\vec v_1) \land (\vec v_1 \ne 0) \rightarrow \text{Rank}(S) = n \\
c = \text{scalar} \\
\vec v = \text{vector} \\
S = \text{subset} 
\end{align*}
$$
---
scalar multiplicity property

- subset without scalar multiple of vector equal linearly independent subset of vector space
---
scalar multiplicity property formula

$$
\begin{align*}
(c_1\vec v_1 + c_2\vec v_2 = \vec 0) \land (\vec v_1 \ne c\vec v_2) \rightarrow \text{Rank}(S) = n \\
c = \text{scalar} \\
\vec v = \text{vector} \\
S = \text{subset}
\end{align*}
$$
---
zero vector property

- subset with at least 1 zero vector equal linearly dependent subset of vector space
---
zero vector property formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\vec 0 \in S \rightarrow \text{Rank}(S) < n \\
S = [\vec v_1, \dots, \vec v_n] \subseteq \mathcal V \\
S = \text{subset} \\
n = \text{number of columns} \\
\vec x = \text{solution} \\
\mathcal V = \text{vector space}
\end{align*}
$$
---
test independence

- generate matrix whose columns equal the vectors of subset of vector space
- form the reduced row echelon of the system
- if number of pivot columns equal number of columns then linearly independent subset
- if number of pivot columns not equal number of columns then linearly dependent subset
---
size property

- for collection of $n$ vectors, each with linear combination of the same $m$ coordinates, if $n = m$ then linear independence
- for collection of $n$ vectors, each with linear combination of the same $n$ coordinates, if $n > m$ then linear dependence
---
linear combination property

- subset with at least two elements and without linear combination of vector equal linearly independent subset of vector space
---
linear combination property formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\not \exists (c_1\vec v_1 + \dots c_n \vec v_n) \in S \rightarrow \text{Rank}(S) = n \\
c = \text{scalar} \\
\vec v = \text{vector} \\
S = \text{subset} \\
n = \text{number of columns}
\end{align*}
$$
---
redundant vector

- vector expressible as linear combination of the other vectors of set
---
redundant vector formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\vec v \leftrightarrow \text{Span}(S) = \text{Span}(S - \{\vec v\})\\
\vec v = \text{redundant vector} \\
S = \text{subset}
\end{align*}
$$
---
redundancy property

- subset without redundant vector equal linearly independent subset of vector space
---
redundancy property formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\nexists \vec v \in S \rightarrow \text{Rank}(S) = n \\
\forall (2 \le k \le n), \vec v_k \not\in \text{Span}(\{\vec v_1 \dots \vec v_{k-1} \}) \\
\vec v = \text{redundant vector} \\
S = \text{subset} \\
n = \text{number of columns}
\end{align*}
$$
---
infinite linear independence

- every finite subset of infinite subset of vector space equal linearly independent subset
---
infinite linear independence formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\text{Rank}(S_1, \dots, S_k) = n \rightarrow \text{Rank}(S) = n \\
S_1 \dots S_n \subseteq S \le \mathcal V \\
S = \text{infinite subset} \\
\mathcal V = \text{vector space} 
\end{align*}
$$
---
existence and uniqueness

- there exists unique linear combination of the vectors of subset for every vector of the span of subset
---
existence and uniqueness formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\forall \vec v \in \text{Span}(S)\exists !(c_1 \vec v_1 + \dots c_k \vec v_k) \rightarrow \text{Rank}(S) = n \\
\vec v = \text{vector} \\
S = \text{subset} \\
c = \text{scalar} \\
n = \text{number of columns}
\end{align*}
$$
