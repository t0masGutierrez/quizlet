absolute value

- nonnegativity operation on real number equal distance between real numbers
---
absolute value formula

$$
\begin{align*}
x \in \mathbb R \rightarrow |x| \ge 0
\end{align*}
$$
---
absolute value property

- negative
- multiplication
- addition
- subtraction
---
absolute value property formula

$$
\begin{align*}
|-x| = |x| \\
|x \cdot y| = (|x|)(|y|) \\
|x + y| \le |x| + |y| \\
||x| + |y|| \le |x - y| \\
|x - y| \le |x| + |y| \\
\end{align*}
$$
---
metric

- distance between coordinates of set
---
metric formula

$$
\begin{align*}
d: X \times X \rightarrow \mathbb R = [0, \infty)
\end{align*}
$$
---
metric space

- set whose distance between coordinates equal real number
---
metric space formula

$$
\begin{align*}
x = y \leftrightarrow \text{d}(x, y) = 0 \\
x \ne y \rightarrow \text{d}(x, y) > 0 \\
\text{d}(x, y) = \text{d}(y, x) \\
\text{d}(x, y) \le \text{d}(x, z) + \text{d}(z, y) 
\end{align*}
$$
---
open ball

- sphere with finite radius from center
---
open ball formula

$$
\begin{align*}
B_r(x_0) = \{x \in X |\text{d}(x, x_0) < r\} \\
x = \text{point} \\
\text{d} = \text{metric} \\
x_0 = \text{center} \\
r = \text{radius}
\end{align*}
$$
---
neighborhood

- region where there exists open ball around center
---
neighborhood formula

$$
\begin{align*}
N \subset X, \exists (r > 0) \in \mathbb R: x_0 \in B_r(x_0) \subset N_r(x_0) \\
N = \text{neighborhood} \\
X = \text{metric space} \\
r = \text{radius} \\
x_0 = \text{center} \\
B = \text{open ball}
\end{align*}
$$
---
limit point

- every neighborhood around limit point contain point of set
---
limit point formula

$$
\begin{align*}
S \subset X, \forall (r > 0) \in \mathbb R: (N_r(x_0) \setminus \{x_0\}) \cap S \ne \emptyset \\
X = \text{metric space} \\
r = \text{radius} \\
N = \text{neighborhood} \\
x_0 = \text{limit point}
\end{align*}
$$
---
interior point

- every point of neighborhood around interior point equal point of set
---
interior point formula

$$
\begin{align*}
S \subset X, \exists (r > 0) \in \mathbb R: N_r(x_0) \subset S \\
X = \text{metric space} \\
r = \text{radius} \\
N = \text{neighborhood} \\
x_0 = \text{interior point}
\end{align*}
$$
---
open

- every point of open set equal interior point of open set
---
open formula

$$
\begin{align*}
S \subset X, \forall x \in S, \exists (r > 0) \in \mathbb R: N_r(x) \subset S \\
S = \text{open set} \\
X = \text{metric space} \\
x = \text{interior point} \\
r = \text{radius} \\
N = \text{neighborhood}
\end{align*}
$$
---
relatively open

- every point of relatively open set equal interior point of relatively open set
---
relatively open formula

$$
\begin{align*}
S \subset Y \subset X, \forall x \in S, \exists (r > 0) \in \mathbb R: N_r(x) \cap Y \subset S \\
S = \text{relatively open set} \\
X = \text{metric space} \\
r = \text{radius} \\
N = \text{neighborhood} \\
x = \text{interior point}
\end{align*}
$$
---
closed

- every limit point of closed set equal point of closed set
---
closed formula

$$
\begin{align*}
S' \subset S \subset X \\
S' = \text{derived set} \\
S = \text{closed set} \\
X = \text{metric space}
\end{align*}
$$
---
perfect

- closed set and every point of perfect set equal limit point of perfect set
---
perfect formula

$$
\begin{align*}
S' = S \subset X \\
S' = \text{derived set} \\
S = \text{perfect set} \\
X = \text{metric space}
\end{align*}
$$
---
closure

- union between set and derived set
---
closure formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\bar S = S' \cup S\\
S' = \text{derived set}
\end{align*}
$$
---
closure property

- derived closure
- equal closure
- sub closure
---
closure property formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\bar S' \subset \bar S \subset X \\
S' \subset S \subset X \leftrightarrow \bar S = S \\
(=S \subset K) \land (K' \subset K \subset X) \rightarrow \bar S \subset K
\end{align*}
$$
---
supremum closure property

- there exists supremum of closure
---
supremum closure property formula

$$
\begin{align*}
S \subset X \rightarrow \exists \sup \bar S \\
\bar S = \text{closure}
\end{align*}
$$
---
dense

- every point of metric space equal point of closure
---
dense formula

$$
\begin{align*}
S \subset X, \forall x \in X: x \in (S \cup S') \\
S = \text{dense set} \\
X = \text{metric space} \\
S' = \text{derived set}
\end{align*}
$$
---
bounded

- every point of bounded set within finite radius from center
---
bounded formula

$$
\begin{align*}
S \subset X, \exists x_0 \in X, \exists (r > 0) \in \mathbb R: S \subset B_r(x_0) \\
S = \text{bounded set} \\
X = \text{metric space} \\
r = \text{radius} \\
x_0 = \text{center} \\
\text{d} = \text{metric}
\end{align*}
$$
---
cover property

- empty set equal both open set and closed set
- metric space equal both open set and closed set
---
cover property formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\emptyset \subset X, \forall x \in \emptyset , \exists r > 0 \in \mathbb R: N_r(x_0) \subset \emptyset, X \\
\emptyset \subset X, \forall r > 0 \in \mathbb R: N_r(x_0) \setminus \{x_0\} \cap \emptyset, X \ne \emptyset
\end{align*}
$$
---
interval limit point property

- every point of interval equal limit point including endpoint
---
interval limit point property

$$
\begin{align*}
(a, b) = S \subset X \rightarrow S' = [a, b] 
\end{align*}
$$
---
infinite limit point property

- every neighborhood around limit point contain infinite point of set
---
infinite limit point property formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\{0, 1, \dots, n\} \not\sim S \subset X \rightarrow \forall (r > 0) \in \mathbb R, \forall x_0 \in S': |N_r(x_0) \setminus \{x_0\} \cap S| = \infty \\
S = \text{infinite set} \\
X = \text{metric space} \\
r = \text{radius} \\
N = \text{neighborhood} \\
x_0 = \text{limit point}
\end{align*}
$$
---
finite limit point property

- every point of finite set equal isolated point of finite set
---
finite limit point property formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\{0, 1, \dots, n\} \sim S \subset X \rightarrow \forall (r > 0) \in \mathbb R, \forall x \in S: N_r(x) \setminus \{x_0\} \cap S = \{x_0\} \\
S = \text{finite set} \\
X = \text{metric space} \\
r = \text{radius} \\
N = \text{neighborhood} \\
x = \text{isolated point}
\end{align*}
$$
---
join complement property

- complement of set union equal intersection of set complement
---
join complement property formula

$$
\begin{align*}
(\bigcup_{i} S_i)^c = \bigcap_{i} (S_i^c) \\
c = \text{complement}
\end{align*}
$$
---
cover complement property

- closed set equal complement of open set
- open set equal complement of closed set
---
cover complement property formula

$$
\begin{align*}
S' \subset S \subset X \leftrightarrow \forall x \in S^c, \exists (r > 0) \in \mathbb R: N_r(x) \subset S^c \\
S \subset X, \forall x \in S, \exists (r > 0) \in \mathbb R: N_r(x) \subset S \leftrightarrow (S')^c \subset S^c \subset X \\
S' = \text{derived set} \\
c = \text{complement} \\
r = \text{radius} \\
N = \text{neighborhood} 
\end{align*}
$$
---
join cover property

- union of open set equal open set
- intersection of closed set equal closed set
- finite intersection of open set equal open set
- finite union of closed set equal closed set
---
join cover property formula

$$
\begin{align*}
S \subset X, \forall x \in S, \exists (r > 0) \in \mathbb R: N_r(x) \subset S \rightarrow N_r(x) \subset \bigcup_{i} S_i \\
S' \subset S \subset X  \rightarrow S' \subset \bigcap_{i} S_i   \\
S \subset X, \forall x \in S, \exists (r > 0) \in \mathbb R: N_r(x) \subset S \rightarrow N_r(x) \subset \bigcap_{i=1}^n S_i \\
S' \subset S \subset X  \rightarrow S' \subset \bigcup_{i}^n S_i  \\
\end{align*}
$$
