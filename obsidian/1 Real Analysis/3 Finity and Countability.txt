segment

- region between two coordinates
---
segment formula

$$
\begin{align*}
[\vec a, \vec b] = \{\lambda \vec a + (1 - \vec b)\lambda: \vec a, \vec b \in \mathbb R^k, \lambda \in [0, 1]\} \\
a = \text{lower coordinate} \\
b = \text{upper coordinate} 
\end{align*}
$$
---
open interval

- region between two exclusive endpoints
---
open interval formula

$$
\begin{align*}
(a, b) = \{x \in \mathbb R| a < x < b\} \\
a = \text{lower endpoint} \\
b = \text{upper endpoint} 
\end{align*}
$$
---
closed interval

- region between two inclusive endpoints
---
closed interval formula

$$
\begin{align*}
[a, b] = \{x \in \mathbb R| a \le x \le b\} \\
a = \text{lower endpoint} \\
b = \text{upper endpoint} 
\end{align*}
$$
---
half interval

- region between inclusive endpoints or exclusive endpoints
---
half interval formula

$$
\begin{align*}
(a, b] = \{x \in \mathbb R| a < x \le b\} \\
[a, b) = \{x \in \mathbb R| a \le x < b\} \\
a = \text{lower endpoint} \\
b = \text{upper endpoint} 
\end{align*}
$$
---
infinite open interval

- region between exclusive endpoint and infinity
---
infinite open interval formula

$$
\begin{align*}
(a, \infty) = \{x \in \mathbb R| x > a\} \\
(-\infty, b) = \{x \in \mathbb R| x < b\}
\end{align*}
$$
---
infinite closed interval

- region between inclusive endpoint and infinity
---
infinite closed interval formula

$$
\begin{align*}
[a, \infty) = \{x \in \mathbb R| x \ge a\} \\
(-\infty, b] = \{x \in \mathbb R| x \le b\}
\end{align*}
$$
---
convex

- every coordinate along segment equal coordinate of convex set
---
convex formula

$$
\begin{align*}
S \subseteq \mathbb R^k, \forall \vec p, \vec q \in S, \forall \lambda \in [0, 1]: \lambda \vec p + (1 - \vec q)\lambda \in S \\
S = \text{convex set}
\end{align*}
$$
---
interval characterization property

- interval equal convex region between endpoints
---
interval characterization property formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\forall x, y \in I: x < y \rightarrow [x, y] = \{z \in \mathbb R| x \le z \le y\} \subseteq I \\
I = \text{interval} 
\end{align*}
$$
---
nested interval

- sequence of subintervals
---
nested interval

$$
\begin{align*}
I_1 \subseteq I_2 \subseteq \dots \subseteq I_n \subseteq I_{n+1} \subseteq \dots \\
I = \text{interval}
\end{align*}
$$
---
nested interval property

- intersection of nested interval equal singleton set
---
nested interval property formula

$$
\begin{align*}
(\forall n \in \mathbb N: I_n = [a_n, b_n]) \land (\lim_{n \rightarrow \infty}b_n - a_n = 0) \rightarrow \exists x \in \mathbb R, \forall n \in \mathbb N: \bigcap_{n=1}^\infty I_n = \{x\} \\
I_n = \text{nested interval} \\
\end{align*}
$$
---
cartesian product

- set of all n-tuples from *n* sets
---
cartesian product formula

$$
\begin{align*}
X \times Y = \{(x, y) | x \in X, y \in Y\} \ne Y \times X
\end{align*}
$$
---
relation

- subset of cartesian product represent relationship between n-tuples from *n* sets
---
relation formula

$$
\begin{align*}
R \subseteq X \times Y
\end{align*}
$$
---
equivalence relation

- reflexive
- symmetric
- transitive
---
equivalence relation formula

$$
\begin{align*}
x R x \\
x R y \rightarrow y R x \\
x R y \land y R z \to x R z \\
\therefore R \sim R
\end{align*}
$$
---
k-cell

- subset of k-dimensional euclidean space equal the cartesian product of interval
---
k-cell formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\forall (a < b) \in \mathbb R: \prod_{i=1}^k [a_i, b_i] = \{\vec q \in \mathbb R^k | a_i \le q_i \le b_i \} \\
a = \text{lower endpoint} \\
b = \text{upper endpoint}
\end{align*}
$$
---
injection

- every element of domain map to 1 element of codomain
- into
---
injection formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\forall a \in A, \forall b \in B: f(a) = f(b) \rightarrow a = b
\end{align*}
$$
---
surjection

- every element of codomain map to $\ge1$ element of domain
- onto
---
surjection formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\forall b \in B, \exists a \in A: f(a) = b
\end{align*}
$$
---
cardinality

- size of set
---
cardinality formula

$$
\begin{align*}
|A| = |B| \leftrightarrow \forall b \in B, \exists !a \in A: f(a) = b
\end{align*}
$$
---
finite

- equal cardinality between set and finite subset of natural numbers
---
finite formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\exists n \in \mathbb N: |S| = |\{0, 1, 2, 3, \dots, n\}| \\
S = \text{finite set}
\end{align*}
$$
---
infinite

- unequal cardinality between set and finite subset of natural numbers
---
infinite formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\not\exists n \in \mathbb N: |S| = |\{0, 1, 2, 3, \dots, n\}| \\
S = \text{infinite set}
\end{align*}
$$
---
countable

- less or equal cardinality between set and natural numbers
---
countable formula

$$
\begin{align*}
|S| \le |\mathbb N| \\
S = \text{countable set}
\end{align*}
$$
---
uncountable

- greater cardinality between set and natural numbers
---
uncountable formula

$$
\begin{align*}
|S| > |\mathbb N| \\
S = \text{uncountable set}
\end{align*}
$$
---
countable example

- natural numbers
- integers
- rational numbers
---
uncountable example

- irrational numbers
- real numbers
---
finite set property

- every finite set equal countable set
---
finite set property formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\exists n \in \mathbb N: |S| = |\{0, 1, 2, 3, \dots, n\}| \rightarrow |S| \le |\mathbb N| \\
S = \text{countable set}
\end{align*}
$$
---
countable subset property

- every subset of countable set equal countable set
---
countable subset property formula

$$
\begin{align*}
(|S| \le |\mathbb N|) \land (S_1 \subseteq S) \rightarrow |S_1| \le |\mathbb N| \\
S = \text{countable set}
\end{align*}
$$
---
countable union property

- every countable union of countable set equal countable set
---
countable union property formula

$$
\begin{align*}
(|S| \le |\mathbb N|) \land (|S_1, S_2, S_3, \dots| \le |\mathbb N|) \rightarrow |\bigcup_{n=1}^\infty S_n| \le |\mathbb N| \\
S = \text{countable set}
\end{align*}
$$
---
uncountable interval property

- every interval of real numbers equal uncountable set
---
uncountable interval property formula

$$
\begin{align*}
[0, 1] = \{x \in \mathbb R| 0 \le x \le 1\} \rightarrow |[0, 1] | > |\mathbb N|
\end{align*}
$$
