sound wave

- pressure oscillations about the surrounding medium pressure
![[Physics/Images/sound wave.png]]
---
compression

- high pressure region
---
rarefaction

- low pressure region
---
particle motion

- particle move right during upward displacement
- particle move left during downward displacement
- pressure most positive during compression
- pressure most negative during rarefaction
- maximum pressure during zero displacement
- minimum pressure during maximum displacement
![[Physics/Images/particle motion.png]]
---
average power

- rate of energy transfer
---
average power formula

$$
\begin{align*}
P_{avg} = \frac{E}{t} \\
E = \text{energy} \\
t = \text{time}
\end{align*}
$$
---
instantaneous power

- rate of doing work
---
instantaneous power formula

$$
\begin{align*}
P = \overrightarrow F \cdot \overrightarrow v \\
F = \text{force} \\
v = \text{velocity}
\end{align*}
$$
---
sound intensity

- loudness
![[Physics/Images/sound intensity.png]]
---
sound intensity formula

$$
\begin{align*}
I = \frac{P}{A} \\
P = \text{power} \\
A = \text{area}
\end{align*}
$$
---
sphere sound intensity

- sound intensity of sphere decreases equally in all directions as distance from sphere increases
![[Physics/Images/sphere sound intensity.png]]
---
sphere sound intensity formula

$$
\begin{align*}
I = \frac{P}{4\pi r^2} \\
P = \text{power} \\
r = \text{distance}
\end{align*}
$$
---
sound intensity level

- logarithmic measure of sound intensity relative reference sound intensity
![[Physics/Images/sound intensity level.png]]
---
sound intensity level formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\beta = 10 \log(\frac{I}{I_0}) \\
I = \text{sound intensity} \\
I_0 = 10^{-12}
\end{align*}
$$
---
sound perception

- factor of 10 sound intensity increase equal multiple of 10 sound intensity level increase
![[Physics/Images/sound perception.png]]
---
sound perception formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\Delta I^n \rightarrow \Delta \beta = 10n \\
I = \text{sound intensity} \\
\beta = \text{sound intensity level}
\end{align*}
$$
---
moving observer sound perception

- observer moving toward stationary source perceive higher frequency than stationary observer
![[Physics/Images/moving observer sound perception.png]]
---
moving observer sound perception formula

$$
\begin{align*}
f_o = (\frac{v \pm v_0}{v})f_s \\
v = \text{343} \\
v_o = \text{observer velocity} \\
f_s = \text{source frequency}
\end{align*}
$$
---
moving source sound perception

- source moving toward stationary observer perceive higher frequency than stationary source
![[Physics/Images/moving source sound perception.png|400]]
---
moving source sound perception formula

$$
\begin{align*}
f_o = (\frac{v}{v \mp v_s})f_s \\
v = 343 \\
v_s = \text{source velocity} \\
f_s = \text{source frequency}
\end{align*}
$$
---
doppler effect

- moving together equal higher frequency and shorter wavelength
- perception of frequency dependent relative motion between observer and source
- moving away equal lower frequency and longer wavelength
![[Physics/Images/doppler effect.png|500]]
---
doppler formula

$$
\begin{align*}
f_o = (\frac{v \pm v_o}{v \mp v_s})f_s \\
v = 343 \\
v_o = \text{observer velocity} \\
v_s = \text{source velocity} \\
f_s = \text{source frequency} \\
\end{align*}
$$

wave front

- separation of wave crest by single wavelength
![[Physics/Images/wave front.png]]
---
interference

- combination of two waves at the same point dependent path difference
![[Physics/Images/source interference.png]]
---
constructive interference formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\Delta r = n\lambda \\
n = Z^+ \\
\lambda = \text{wavelength}
\end{align*}
$$
---
destructive interference formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\Delta r = (n + \frac{1}{2})\lambda \\
n = Z^+ \\
\lambda = \text{wavelength}
\end{align*}
$$
---
beat frequency

- interfering sound waves of different frequency oscillate between in phase and out phase
![[Physics/Images/beat frequency.png]]
---
beat frequency formula

$$
\begin{align*}
f = |f_2 - f_1| \\
f_2 = \text{reflect frequency} \\
f_1 = \text{incident frequency}
\end{align*}
$$
---
asymmetric normal mode

- possible asymmetric standing wave pattern where all particles oscillate with the same frequency
![[Physics/Images/asymmetric normal mode.png|400]]
---
asymmetric resonant wavelength

- wavelength of asymmetric normal mode
---
asymmetric resonant wavelength formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\lambda_n = \frac{4L}{n} \\
L = \text{length} \\
n = 1, 3, 5, ...
\end{align*}
$$
---
asymmetric resonant frequency

- odd integer multiple of fundamental frequency
---
asymmetric resonant frequency formula

$$
\begin{align*}
f_n = \frac{nv}{4L} \\
n = 1, 3, 5, ... \\
v = \text{wave velocity} \\
L = \text{length}
\end{align*}
$$
