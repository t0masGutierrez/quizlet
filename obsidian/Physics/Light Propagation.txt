wavefront

- imaginary perpendicular surface intersecting all waves in phase
![[Physics/Images/wavefront1.png|300]]
---
wave velocity

- rate of wavefront
![[Physics/Images/wave velocity.png|300]]
---
wave velocity formula

$$
\begin{align*}
v = \lambda f = \frac{\omega}{k} \\
c = \sqrt{\frac{1}{\epsilon_0 \mu_0}} = 3 \times 10^{8} \\
\lambda = \text{wavelength} \\
f = \text{oscillation frequency} \\
\omega = \text{angular frequency} \\
k = \text{wave number} \\
\epsilon_0 = 8.85 \times 10^{-12} \\
\mu_0 = 4\pi \times 10^{-7} \\
\end{align*}
$$
---
refraction index

- ratio between vacuum and non vacuum
![[Physics/Images/refraction index.png]]
---
refraction index formula

$$
\begin{align*}
n = \frac{c}{v} = \frac{\lambda_0}{\lambda} = \sqrt{KK_m} \\
c = 3 \times 10^8 \\
v = \text{wave velocity} \\
\lambda = \text{wavelength} \\
K = \text{dielectric constant} \\
K_m = \text{relative permeability} 
\end{align*}
$$

---
law of reflection

- equal angle between incidence and reflection with respect to the normal surface
![[Physics/Images/law of reflection.png|300]]
---
formula of reflection

$$
\begin{align*}
\theta_1 = \theta_r \\
\theta_1 = \text{angular incidence} \\
\theta_r = \text{angular reflection}
\end{align*}
$$
---
refraction

- change of direction after the transmission of light through surface
![[Physics/Images/refraction.png|300]]
---
law of refraction

- angular refraction dependent refraction index
![[Physics/Images/law of refraction.png]]
---
formula of refraction

$$
\begin{align*}
n_1\sin(\theta_1) = n_2\sin(\theta_2) \\
n = \text{refraction index} \\
\theta_1 = \text{angular incidence} \\
\theta_2 = \text{angular refraction}
\end{align*}
$$
---
critical angle

- angular incidence where angular refraction equal 90
![[Physics/Images/critical angle.png]]
---
critical angle formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\theta_c = \arcsin(\frac{n_2}{n_1}) \\
n_2 < n_1 \\
n = \text{refraction index}
\end{align*}
$$

malus law

- intensity of electromagnetic wave after transmission through polarizer
![[Physics/Images/malus law.png|400]]
---
malus formula

$$
\begin{align*}
I = I_0 \cos^2(\theta) \\
I = \text{intensity} \\
\theta = \text{angular position}
\end{align*}
$$
---
calculate malus law

- intensity of polar electromagnetic wave directly proportional square of electric field amplitude
- intensity of non polar electromagnetic wave equal half of maximum intensity
- angular position of 2nd polarizer with respect to 1st axis
---
polarization by reflection

- electric field perpendicular incidence plane equal partial horizontal polarization parallel incidence surface
- electric field parallel incidence plane equal partial vertical polarization perpendicular incidence surface
![[Physics/Images/polarization by reflection.png|400]]
---
brewsters law

- non polar angular incidence where reflection equal full perpendicular polarization and refraction equal partial parallel polarization
- polar angular incidence where reflection equal zero and refraction equal full parallel polarization
![[Physics/Images/brewsters law.png]]
---
brewsters formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\theta_1 = \arctan(\frac{n_2}{n_1}) \\
\theta_1 + \theta_2 = 90 \\
n = \text{refraction index}
\end{align*}
$$

---
scattering

- absorption and reradiation of light across different directions
![[Physics/Images/scattering.png|300]]
---
polarization by scattering

- ray perpendicular electric field radiate strongly
- ray parallel electric field radiate weakly
![[Physics/Images/polarization by scattering.png|400]]
---
polarization by scattering formula

$$
\begin{align*}
I \propto f^4 \propto \frac{1}{\lambda^4} \\
I = \text{intensity} \\
f = \text{oscillation frequency} \\
\lambda = \text{wavelength}
\end{align*}
$$
