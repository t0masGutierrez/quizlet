magnetic flux

- amount of magnetic field flowing through normal surface
![[Physics/Images/magnetic flux.png|300]]
---
magnetic flux formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\Phi = \overrightarrow B \cdot \overrightarrow A = BA \cos(\theta) \\
B = \text{magnetic field} \\
A = \text{area} \\
\theta = \text{angular position}
\end{align*}
$$
---
generating emf

- change magnetic field
- relative movement between magnetic field and conductor
- change area
---
faradays law

- induced emf directly proportional negative rate of change of magnetic flux
![[Physics/Images/faradays law.png|400]]
---
faradays formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\epsilon = N\frac{-d\Phi}{dt} = N\frac{-d}{dt} \int_{A_1}^{A_2} \overrightarrow B \cdot d\overrightarrow A \\
N = \text{number of loops} \\
\Phi = \text{magnetic flux} \\
t = \text{time} \\
B = \text{magnetic field} \\
A = \text{area}
\end{align*}
$$
---
calculate faradays law

- change magnetic flux
- induced emf create electric field
- electric field exert force on electric charge
- acceleration of electric charge generate electric current
---
lenz law

- direction of induced emf equal opposite change of magnetic flux
![[Physics/Images/lenz law.png|500]]
---
calculate lenz law

- increasing magnetic flux equal negative emf
- decreasing magnetic flux equal positive emf
- emf direction equal magnetic field direction
---
uniform motional emf

- generate emf by moving uniform conductor through magnetic field
![[Physics/Images/uniform motional emf.png]]
---
uniform motional emf formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\epsilon = BL\frac{dx}{dt} = BLv \\
B = \text{magnetic field} \\
L = \text{length} \\
x = \text{position} \\
t = \text{time} \\
v = \text{velocity}
\end{align*}
$$
---
non uniform motional emf

- generate emf by moving non uniform conductor through magnetic field
![[Physics/Images/non uniform motional emf.png|250]]
---
non uniform motional emf formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\epsilon = \oint_{L_1}^{L_2} (\overrightarrow v \times \overrightarrow B) \cdot d\overrightarrow L \\
v = \text{velocity} \\
B = \text{magnetic field} \\
L = \text{length}
\end{align*}
$$
---
static electric field

- work done by conservative force on electric charge  
![[Physics/Images/static electric field.png]]
---
static electric field formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\oint_{L_1}^{L_2} \overrightarrow E \cdot d \overrightarrow L = 0 \\
E = \text{electric field} \\
L = \text{length}
\end{align*}
$$
---
induced electric field

- work done by non conservative force on electric charge
![[Physics/Images/induced electric field.png]]
---
induced electric field formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\oint_{L_1}^{L_2} \overrightarrow E \cdot d \overrightarrow L = \frac{-d\Phi}{dt} \\
E = \text{electric field} \\
L = \text{length} \\
\Phi = \text{magnetic flux}
\end{align*}
$$
---
eddy current

- circulating loop of induced electric current
![[Physics/Images/eddy current.png|500]]
---
eddy current formula

$$
\begin{align*}
I = \frac{\epsilon}{R} \\
\epsilon = \text{emf} \\
R = \text{resistance}
\end{align*}
$$
---
conduction current

- rate of electric charge flow due to electric field
---
conduction current formula

$$
\begin{align*}
I_c = \int_{A_1}^{A_2} \overrightarrow J \cdot d \overrightarrow A \\
J = \text{electric current density} \\
A = \text{area}
\end{align*}
$$
---
displacement current

- rate of fictitious electric charge flow due to changing electric field
---
displacement current formula

$$
\begin{align*}
I_d = \epsilon_0 \frac{d\Phi}{dt} \\
\epsilon_0 = 8.85 \times 10^{-12} \\
\Phi = \text{electric flux}
\end{align*}
$$
---
amperes law

- magnetic field around amperian loop directly proportional net electric current inside amperian loop
![[Physics/Images/amperes law1.png|300]]
---
amperes formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\oint_{L_1}^{L_2} \overrightarrow B \cdot d\overrightarrow L = \mu_0 (I_c + I
_d)_{enc} \\
B = \text{magnetic field} \\
L = \text{length} \\
\mu_0 = 4\pi \times 10^{-7} \\
I_c = \text{conduction current} \\
I_d = \text{displacement current}
\end{align*}
$$
---
electromagnetism

- electric flux directly proportional net electric charge inside gaussian surface 
- magnetic flux inside gaussian surface equal zero
- changing magnetic flux generate electric field
- changing electric flux generate magnetic field
![[Physics/Images/electromagnetism.png|300]]
---
electromagnetism formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\oint_{A_1}^{A_2}\overrightarrow E \cdot d\overrightarrow A = \frac{Q_{enc}}{\epsilon_0} \\
\oint_{A_1}^{A_2}\overrightarrow B \cdot d\overrightarrow A = 0 \\
\oint_{L_1}^{L_2} \overrightarrow E \cdot d \overrightarrow L = \frac{-d\Phi_B}{dt} \\
\oint_{L_1}^{L_2} \overrightarrow B \cdot d\overrightarrow L = \mu_0 (I_c + \epsilon_0 \frac{d\Phi_E}{dt})_{enc} \\
\end{align*}
$$
---
lorentz force

- net electromagnetic force 
---
lorentz force formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\overrightarrow F_{net} = q(\overrightarrow E + \overrightarrow v \times \overrightarrow B) \\
q = \text{electric charge} \\
E = \text{electric field} \\
v = \text{velocity} \\
B = \text{magnetic field}
\end{align*}
$$
---
electric generator

- conversion of mechanical energy into electrical energy by inducing emf with rotating coil inside magnetic field
![[Physics/Images/electric generator.png|400]]
---
electric generator formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\epsilon = \epsilon_0 \sin(\omega t) = NBA \omega \sin(\omega t) \\
\epsilon_0 = \text{amplitude} \\
\omega = \text{angular frequency} \\
t = \text{time} \\
N = \text{number of loops} \\
B = \text{magnetic field} \\
A = \text{area}
\end{align*}
$$
---
electric motor

- conversion of electrical energy into mechanical energy
![[Physics/Images/electric motor.png]]
---
electric motor formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\epsilon = \epsilon_0 - \epsilon_{induced} \\
\epsilon = \text{emf}
\end{align*}
$$
