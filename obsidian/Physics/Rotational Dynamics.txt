torque

- rotational analogue of force
![[Physics/Images/torque.png]]
---
torque formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\overrightarrow \tau = \overrightarrow r \times \overrightarrow F = rF \sin(\theta) \\
r = \text{radius} \\
F = \text{force} \\
\theta = \text{angle between vectors}
\end{align*}
$$
---
tangential force torque

- component of force perpendicular radius
![[Physics/Images/tangential force torque.png]]
---
tangential force torque formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\tau = r F\perp \\
r = \text{radius} \\
F = \text{force}
\end{align*}
$$
---
lever arm torque

- perpendicular distance from the axis of rotation to the line of action of the force
![[Physics/Images/lever arm torque.png]]
---
lever arm torque formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\tau = r\perp F \\
r = \text{radius} \\
F = \text{force}
\end{align*}
$$
---
newtons second law

- angular acceleration dependent moment of inertia and amount of net external torque acting on moment of inertia
![[Physics/Images/newtons second law.png]]
---
newton 2nd formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\overrightarrow \tau_{net} = I \overrightarrow \alpha \\
I = \text{moment of inertia} \\
\alpha = \text{angular acceleration}
\end{align*}
$$
---
hinge

- connection of two objects such that normal force prevent translational motion but allow rotational motion
![[Physics/Images/hinge.png]]
---
work

- transfer of energy from the application of torque causing displacement parallel torque
![[Physics/Images/work.png]]
---
constant work formula

$$
\begin{align*}
W = \overrightarrow \tau \cdot d \overrightarrow \theta = \tau \Delta \theta \cos(\phi) \\
\tau = \text{torque} \\
\theta = \text{angular position} \\
\phi = \text{angle between vectors}
\end{align*}
$$
---
variable work formula

$$
\begin{align*}
W = \sum_i \tau_i \Delta \theta_i = \int_{\theta_1}^{\theta_2} \overrightarrow \tau \cdot d \overrightarrow \theta \\
\tau = \text{torque} \\
\theta = \text{angular position}
\end{align*}
$$
---
rotational kinetic energy

- energy of rotational motion
---
rotational kinetic energy formula

$$
\begin{align*}
K = \frac{1}{2}I\omega^2 \\
I = \text{moment of inertia} \\
\omega = \text{angular velocity}
\end{align*}
$$
---
net work

- work done by net torque acting on object
---
net work formula

$$
\begin{align*}
W_{net} = \overrightarrow \tau_{net} \cdot d \overrightarrow \theta = I\frac{d \overrightarrow \omega}{dt} \cdot d \overrightarrow \theta = I\overrightarrow \omega \cdot d \overrightarrow \omega \\
W_{net} = I \int_{\omega_1}^{\omega_2} \overrightarrow \omega \cdot d \overrightarrow \omega = \frac{1}{2}I\omega^2 \\
\tau = \text{torque} \\
\theta = \text{angular position} \\
I = \text{moment of inertia} \\
\omega = \text{angular velocity} \\
t = \text{time}
\end{align*}
$$
---
work energy

- work done by net torque equal change of rotational kinetic energy
---
work energy formula

$$
\begin{align*}
W_{net} = K_f - K_i \\
K = \text{kinetic energy}
\end{align*}
$$
---
rolling kinetic energy

- rolling kinetic energy equal translational kinetic energy and rotational kinetic energy
![[Physics/Images/kinetic energy.png]]
---
rolling kinetic energy formula

$$
\begin{align*}
K = \frac{1}{2}mv_{cm}^2 + \frac{1}{2}I_{cm}\omega^2 \\
m = \text{mass} \\
v = \text{velocity} \\
I = \text{moment of inertia} \\
\omega = \text{angular velocity}
\end{align*}
$$
---
rolling without slipping

- stationary point of contact between rolling object and surface
- static friction provide the necessary torque
---
rolling without slipping formula

$$
\begin{align*}
r_{cm} = R\theta \\
v_{cm} = R\omega \\
a_{cm} = R\alpha \\
R = \text{radius} \\
\theta = \text{angular position} \\
\omega = \text{angular velocity} \\
\alpha = \text{angular acceleration}
\end{align*}
$$
---
rolling with slipping

- moving point of contact between rolling object and surface
- kinetic friction provide the necessary torque
---
rolling with slipping formula

$$
\begin{align*}
r = r_{cm} - R\theta \\
v = v_{cm} - R\omega \\
a = a_{cm} -  R\alpha \\
R = \text{radius} \\
\theta = \text{angular position} \\
\omega = \text{angular velocity} \\
\alpha = \text{angular acceleration}
\end{align*}
$$
---
work energy

- work done by rolling with slipping equal non zero change of mechanical energy
---
work energy formula

$$
\begin{align*}
W_{noncons} = \Delta E \\
W_{noncons} = \Delta K + \Delta U \\
E = \text{mechanical energy} \\
K = \text{kinetic energy} \\
U = \text{potential energy}
\end{align*}
$$
---
energy conservation

- work done by rolling without slipping equal zero change of mechanical energy
---
energy conservation formula

$$
\begin{align*}
E_i = E_f \\
\Delta K = - \Delta U \\
E = \text{mechanical energy} \\
K = \text{kinetic energy} \\
U = \text{potential energy}
\end{align*}
$$
---
angular momentum

- difficulty of stopping or changing direction of rotational motion
![[Physics/Images/angular momentum.png]]
---
angular momentum formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\overrightarrow L = \overrightarrow r \times \overrightarrow p = I \overrightarrow \omega \\
r = \text{radius} \\
p = \text{momentum} \\
I = \text{moment of inertia} \\
\omega = \text{angular velocity}
\end{align*}
$$
---
calculate angular momentum

- equal direction of angular momentum and angular impulse
---
newtons second law

- angular acceleration dependent moment of inertia and net external torque acting on moment of inertia
---
newtons 2nd formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\overrightarrow \tau_{net} = \frac{d\overrightarrow L}{dt} \\
L = \text{angular momentum} \\
t = \text{time}
\end{align*}
$$
---
angular impulse

- measure of net external torque over time
![[Physics/Images/impulse.png]]
---
angular impulse formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\overrightarrow H = \sum \tau_{avg} \Delta t = \int_{t_1}^{t_2} \overrightarrow \tau \cdot d \overrightarrow t \\
\tau_{avg} = \text{average torque} \\
t = \text{time} \\
\tau_{net} = \text{net torque}
\end{align*}
$$
---
impulse momentum

- angular impulse equal change of angular momentum
---
impulse momentum formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\overrightarrow H = \Delta \overrightarrow L = I \Delta \overrightarrow \omega \\
L = \text{angular momentum} \\
I = \text{moment of inertia} \\
\omega = \text{angular velocity}
\end{align*}
$$
---
angular momentum conservation

- zero net external torque equal zero change of angular momentum
---
angular momentum conservation formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\overrightarrow L_i = \overrightarrow L_f \\
\end{align*}
$$
---
precession

- circular motion of axis of rotation and rotational motion about axis of rotation
![[Physics/Images/precession.png]]
---
precession formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\Omega = \frac{wr}{I\omega} \\
w = \text{weight} \\
r = \text{radius} \\
I = \text{moment of inertia} \\
\omega = \text{angular velocity}
\end{align*}
$$
---
static equilibrium

- object at rest
---
dynamic equilibrium

- object in motion with constant velocity
---
translational equilibrium

- zero net force and zero acceleration
---
translational equilibrium formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\overrightarrow F_{net} = 0 \\
\overrightarrow a = 0
\end{align*}
$$
---
rotational equilibrium

- zero net torque and zero angular acceleration
---
rotational equilibrium formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\overrightarrow \tau_{net} = 0 \\
\overrightarrow \alpha = 0
\end{align*}
$$
