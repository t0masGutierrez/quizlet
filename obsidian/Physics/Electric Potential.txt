work

- transfer of energy from the application of force causing displacement parallel force
![[Physics/Images/net work.png]]
---
constant work formula

$$
\begin{align*}
W = \overrightarrow F \cdot d \overrightarrow r = F \Delta r \cos(\theta) \\
F = \text{force} \\
r = \text{position} \\
\theta = \text{angular position}
\end{align*}
$$
---
variable work formula

$$
\begin{align*}
W = \sum_i F_i \Delta r_i = \int_{r_1}^{r_2} \overrightarrow F \cdot d \overrightarrow r \\
F = \text{force} \\
r = \text{position}
\end{align*}
$$
---
calculate work

- displacement parallel force equal positive work
- displacement anti parallel force equal negative work
- displacement perpendicular force equal zero work
---
potential energy

- energy of position
![[Physics/Images/potential energy.png]]
---
potential energy formula

$$
\begin{align*}
U = \int_{r_2}^{r_1} \overrightarrow F_{cons} \cdot d \overrightarrow r \\
F_{cons} = \text{conservative force} \\
r = \text{position}
\end{align*}
$$
---
conservative force

- reversible force that conserve mechanical energy
- dependent position and independent path
![[Physics/Images/electric conservative force.png|300]]
---
work energy

- work done by conservative force equal negative change of potential energy
---
work energy formula

$$
\begin{align*}
W_{cons} = U_i - U_f \\
U = \text{potential energy}
\end{align*}
$$
---
electric potential energy

- potential energy of electric charge as function of distance
![[Physics/Images/electric potential energy1.png]]
---
electric potential energy formula

$$
\begin{align*}
U = k\frac{q_1 q_2}{r} \\
k = 9 \times 10^9 \\
q = \text{electric charge} \\
r = \text{distance} 
\end{align*}
$$
---
calculate electric potential energy

- positive electric potential energy between same electric charge
- negative electric potential energy between opposite electric charge
---
electric potential energy

- potential energy of electric field as function of position
![[Physics/Images/electric potential energy.png]]
---
electric potential energy formula

$$
\begin{align*}
U = qEy \\
q = \text{electric charge} \\
E = \text{electric field} \\
y = \text{position}
\end{align*}
$$
---
calculate electric potential energy

- electric potential energy decreases parallel electric force
- electric potential energy increases anti parallel electric force
---
electric potential

- region of space where electric charge experience electric potential energy
- negative derivative of electric field
![[Physics/Images/electric potential.png|400]]
---
electric potential formula

$$
\begin{align*}
V = \frac{U}{q} = k\sum_i\frac{Q_i}{r_i} \\
U = \text{potential energy} \\
q = \text{test electric charge} \\
k = 9 \times 10^9 \\
Q = \text{source electric charge} \\
r = \text{distance} 
\end{align*}
$$
---
calculate electric potential

- positive electric charge move from high electric potential to low electric potential
- negative electric charge move from low electric potential to high electric potential
---
equipotential surface

- equal electric potential for every point on surface
- zero work done by electric force on electric charge for every point on surface
![[Physics/Images/equipotential surface.png|300]]
---
calculate equipotential surface

- equipotential surface perpendicular electric field line
- equipotential surface density directly proportional magnitude of electric field
---
electric potential difference

- region of space where electric charge experience change of electric potential energy
- negative anti derivative of electric field
![[Physics/Images/electric potential difference.png]]
---
electric potential difference formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\Delta V = \frac{\Delta U}{q} = k\int \frac{dQ}{r} \\
U = \text{potential energy} \\
q = \text{test electric charge} \\
k = 9 \times 10^9 \\
Q = \text{source electric charge} \\
r = \text{distance} 
\end{align*}
$$
---
calculate electric potential difference

- decreasing voltage parallel electric field
- increasing voltage anti parallel electric field
---
electron volt

- electron change of energy whilst moving through electric potential difference of 1 volt
---
electron volt formula

$$
\begin{align*}
eV = 1.6 \times 10^{-19}
\end{align*}
$$
---
uniform electric field

- region of space around source electric charge where test electric charge experience uniform electric force
![[Physics/Images/uniform electric field.png]]
---
uniform electric field formula

$$
\begin{align*}
E = \frac{\Delta V}{r} \\
V = \text{electric potential} \\
r = \text{distance}
\end{align*}
$$
---
non uniform electric field

- region of space around source electric charge where test electric charge experience non uniform electric force
![[Physics/Images/non uniform electric field.png|400]]
---
non uniform electric field formula

$$
\begin{align*}
E = \frac{-dV}{dr} \\
V = \text{electric potential} \\
r = \text{distance}
\end{align*}
$$
---
electrostatic conductor

- equipotential for every point outer closed surface of electrostatic conductor
- equipotential for every point inner closed surface of electrostatic conductor
![[Physics/Images/electrostatic conductor1.png|400]]
---
electrostatic conductor formula

$$
\begin{align*}
E \perp = \frac{\sigma}{\epsilon_0} \\
\sigma = \text{surface density} \\
\epsilon_0 = 8.85 \times 10^{-12}
\end{align*}
$$
