capacitor

- electric component designed to store electric charge
![[Physics/Images/capacitor.png|200]]
---
capacitance

- storage of electric charge
---
capacitance formula

$$
\begin{align*}
C = \frac{Q}{V} \\
Q = \text{electric charge} \\
V = \text{voltage}
\end{align*}
$$
---
calculate capacitance

- capacitance inversely proportional distance between capacitor
- capacitance directly proportional size of capacitor
---
parallel planar capacitor

- separation of parallel planar conductors with insulator
![[Physics/Images/parallel planar capacitor.png|200]]
---
parallel planar capacitance formula

$$
\begin{align*}
C = \epsilon_0 \frac{A}{r} \\
\epsilon_0 = 8.85 \times 10^{-12} \\
A = \text{area} \\
r = \text{distance}
\end{align*}
$$
---
cylindrical capacitor

- separation of two cylindrical conductors with insulator
![[Physics/Images/cylindrical capacitor.png|400]]
---
cylindrical capacitance formula

$$
\begin{align*}
C = 2\pi \epsilon_0\frac{L}{\ln(\frac{R_2}{R_1})} \\
\epsilon_0 = 8.85 \times 10^{-12} \\
L = \text{length} \\
R = \text{radius}
\end{align*}
$$
---
spherical capacitor

- separation of two spherical conductors with insulator
![[Physics/Images/spherical capacitor.png]]
---
spherical capacitance formula

$$
\begin{align*}
C = 4\pi \epsilon_0 \frac{R_1R_2}{R_2 - R_1} \\
\epsilon_0 = 8.85 \times 10^{-12} \\
R = \text{radius}
\end{align*}
$$
---
series capacitor

- equivalent electric charge equal electric charge across series
- equivalent voltage equal sum of voltage across series
![[Physics/Images/series capacitor.png|300]]
---
series capacitance formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\frac{1}{C_{eq}} = \sum_i \frac{1}{C_i} \\
C = \text{capacitance}
\end{align*}
$$
---
parallel capacitor

- equivalent voltage equal voltage across parallel
- equivalent electric charge equal sum of electric charge across parallel
![[Physics/Images/parallel capacitor.png|300]]
---
parallel capacitance formula

$$
\begin{align*}
C_{eq} = \sum_i C_i \\
C = \text{capacitance}
\end{align*}
$$
---
combination capacitor

- capacitors that do not provide choice between path equal series capacitor
- capacitors that do provide choice between path equal parallel capacitor
---
electric potential energy

- energy of position inside capacitor
---
electric potential energy formula

$$
\begin{align*}
U = \frac{QV}{2} = \frac{CV^2}{2} = \frac{Q^2}{2C} \\
Q = \text{electric charge} \\
V = \text{voltage} \\
C = \text{capacitance}
\end{align*}
$$
---
energy density

- measure of electric potential energy compactness
---
energy density formula

$$
\begin{align*}
u = \frac{1}{2}\epsilon_0 E^2 \\
\epsilon_0 = 8.85 \times 10^{-12} \\
E = \text{electric field}
\end{align*}
$$

dielectric breakdown

- ionization of molecule transform dielectric into conductor
---
dielectric constant

- ratio between dielectric and non dielectric
![[Physics/Images/dielectric constant.png|500]]
---
dielectric constant formula

$$
\begin{align*}
K = \frac{\epsilon}{\epsilon_0} = \frac{C}{C_0} =  \frac{Q}{Q_0} = \frac{V_0}{V} \\
\epsilon = \text{permittivity} \\
C = \text{capacitance} \\
Q = \text{electric charge} \\
V = \text{voltage}
\end{align*}
$$
---
calculate dielectric constant

- dielectric increase capacitance
- dielectric increase electric charge
- dielectric decrease voltage
---
gauss law

- electric flux directly proportional net unbound electric charge inside gaussian surface
![[Physics/Images/gauss law.png|200]]
---
gauss formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\pm \Phi = K\oint_{A_1}^{A_2}\overrightarrow E \cdot d\overrightarrow A = \frac{\pm Q_{enc}}{\epsilon_0} \\
K = \text{dielectric constant} \\
E = \text{electric field} \\
A = \text{area} \\
Q = \text{electric charge} \\
\epsilon_0 = 8.85 \times 10^{-12} \\
\end{align*}
$$
