heat

- transfer of thermal energy between systems of different temperature
![[8 Physics/Images/heat.png|300]]
---
convection

- heat transfer as result of fluid motion
---
conduction

- heat transfer as result of physical contact
---
radiation

- heat transfer as result of electromagnetic waves
---
conduction formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\frac{dQ}{dt} = \frac{kA\Delta T_k}{L} \\
k = \text{conductivity coefficient} \\
A = \text{area} \\
T_k = \text{kelvin} \\
L = \text{length}
\end{align*}
$$
---
radiation formula

$$
\begin{align*}
P = \sigma Ae(T^4 - T_0^4) \\
\sigma = 5.67 \times 10^{-8} \\
A = \text{area} \\
e = \text{emmisivity coefficient} \\
T = \text{temperature}
\end{align*}
$$
---
conductivity

- high conductivity equal conductor
- low conductivity equal insulator
![[8 Physics/Images/conductivity.png]]
---
emissivity

- high emissivity equal high efficiency of radiation emission
- low emissivity equal low efficiency of radiation emission
---
newtons law of cooling

- rate of heat loss directly proportional temperature difference between system and surrounding
![[8 Physics/Images/newtons law of cooling.png]]
---
newtons formula of cooling

$$
\begin{align*}
\frac{dT}{dt} = k(T - T_0) \\
k = \text{cooling constant} \\
T = \text{system temperature} \\
T_0 = \text{surrounding temperature}
\end{align*}
$$
---
boyles law

- if constant temperature and constant number of moles then pressure inversely proportional volume
- isothermal
![[8 Physics/Images/boyles law.png]]
---
boyles formula

$$
\begin{align*}
p_1V_1 = p_2V_2 \\
p = \text{pressure} \\
V = \text{volume} 
\end{align*}
$$
---
charles law

- if constant pressure and constant number of moles then volume directly proportional temperature
- isobaric
![[8 Physics/Images/charles law.png]]
---
charles formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\frac{V_1}{T_1} = \frac{V_2}{T_2} \\
V = \text{volume} \\
T = \text{temperature}
\end{align*}
$$
---
avagadros law

- if constant temperature and constant pressure then volume directly proportional number of moles
- isothermal and isobaric
![[8 Physics/Images/avagadros law.png|400]]
---
avagadros formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\frac{V_1}{n_1} = \frac{V_2}{n_2} \\
V = \text{volume} \\
n = \text{number of moles}
\end{align*}
$$
---
ideal gas

- large number of identical molecules that move randomly with translational motion and undergo elastic collisions
![[8 Physics/Images/ideal gas.png]]
---
microscopic ideal gas formula

$$
\begin{align*}
pV = Nk_bT_k \\
N = \text{number of molecules} \\
k_b = 1.38 \times 10^{-23} \\
T_k = \text{kelvin}
\end{align*}
$$
---
macroscopic ideal gas formula

$$
\begin{align*}
pV = nRT_k \\
n = \text{number of moles} \\
R = 8.31 \\
T_k = \text{kelvin}
\end{align*}
$$
---
ideal gas conversion formula

$$
\begin{align*}
R = k_bN_A \\
k_b = 1.38 \times 10^{-23} \\
N_A = 6.02 \times 10^{23}
\end{align*}
$$
---
molar mass

- amount of mass per mole
---
molar mass formula

$$
\begin{align*}
M = \frac{m}{n} \\
m = \text{mass} \\
n = \text{number of moles}
\end{align*}
$$
---
avagadros number

- number of molecules per mole
---
avagadros formula

$$
\begin{align*}
N_A = \frac{N}{n}= 6.02 \times 10^{23} \\
N = \text{number of molecules} \\
n = \text{number of moles}
\end{align*}
$$
---
number density

- measure of molecular compactness
---
number density formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\rho = \frac{N}{V} \\
N = \text{number of molecules} \\
V = \text{volume}
\end{align*}
$$
---
temperature

- average translational kinetic energy of molecules
![[8 Physics/Images/temperature.png|400]]
---
average translational kinetic energy

- mean kinetic energy of translational motion
---
average translational kinetic energy formula

$$
\begin{align*}
K_{avg} = \frac{3}{2}k_bT_k = \frac{1}{2}mv_{rms}^2 \\
k_b = 1.38 \times 10^{-23} \\
T_k = \text{kelvin} \\
v_{rms} = \text{rms velocity} \\
m = \text{mass} \\
v = \text{velocity}
\end{align*}
$$
---
rms velocity

- square root of the average of the square of the velocity
---
rms velocity formula

$$
\begin{align*}
v_{rms} = \sqrt{\frac{3k_bT_k}{m}} = \sqrt{\frac{3RT_k}{M}} \\
k_b = 1.38 \times 10^{-23} \\
T_k = \text{kelvin} \\
m = \text{mass} \\
R = 8.31 \\
M = \text{molar mass} \\

\end{align*}
$$
---
molecular velocity

- non uniform distribution of molecular velocity
![[8 Physics/Images/molecular velocity.png]]
---
molecular velocity formula

$$
\begin{align*}
f(v) = \frac{4}{\sqrt\pi{}}(\frac{m}{2k_bT_k})^{\frac{3}{2}}v^2e^{(\frac{-mv^2}{2k_bT_k})} \\
m = \text{mass} \\
k_b = 1.38 \times 10^{-23} \\
T_k = \text{kelvin} \\
v = \text{velocity} \\

\end{align*}
$$
---
internal energy

- kinetic energy of molecular motion
![[8 Physics/Images/average kinetic energy.png]]
---
internal energy formula

$$
\begin{align*}
E = \frac{f}{2}Nk_bT_k = \frac{f}{2}nRT_k\\
f = \text{degrees of freedom} \\
N = \text{number of molecules} \\
k_b = 1.38 \times 10^{-23} \\
T_k = \text{kelvin} \\
n = \text{number of moles} \\
R = 8.31
\end{align*}
$$
---
equipartition of energy

- average kinetic energy per molecule of ideal gas equal $\frac{1}{2}k_bT$ for every degree of freedom
- average kinetic energy per mole of ideal gas equal $\frac{1}{2}RT$ for every degree of freedom
---
degrees of freedom

- number of velocity components that describe molecular motion
- translational rotational vibrational
![[8 Physics/Images/degrees of freedom.png]]
---
degrees of freedom formula

$$
\begin{align*}
f_1 = 3_t \\
f_2 = 3_t + 2_r \\
t = \text{translation} \\
r = \text{rotation}
\end{align*}
$$
---
specific heat

- amount of heat that must enter substance to change temperature by $\Delta T$ 
- high specific heat resist temperature change more than low specific heat
---
specific heat formula

$$
\begin{align*}
Q = nc\Delta T_k \\
n = \text{number of moles} \\
c = \text{specific heat capacity} \\
T_k = \text{kelvin}
\end{align*}
$$
---
constant volume specific heat capacity

- specific heat of gas inside rigid container
![[8 Physics/Images/constant volume specific heat capacity.png]]
---
constant volume specific heat capacity formula

$$
\begin{align*}
c_v = \frac{f}{2}R \\
f = \text{degrees of freedom} \\
R = 8.31
\end{align*}
$$
