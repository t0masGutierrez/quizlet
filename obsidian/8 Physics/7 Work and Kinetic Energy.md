### work
- transfer of energy from the application of force causing displacement parallel force
![[8 Physics/Images/work.png|300]]
---
### constant work formula

$$
\begin{aligned}
W = \overrightarrow F \cdot  d\overrightarrow r = F \Delta r \cos(\theta) \\
F = \text{force} \\
r = \text{position} \\
\theta = \text{angle between vectors}
\end{aligned}
$$

---
### variable work formula

$$
\begin{aligned}
W = \sum_i F_i \Delta r_i = \int_{r_1}^{r_2} \overrightarrow F \cdot d \overrightarrow r \\
F = \text{force} \\
r = \text{position}
\end{aligned}
$$

---
### calculate work
- displacement parallel force equal positive work
- displacement anti parallel force equal negative work
- displacement perpendicular force equal zero work
---
### hookes law
- restoring force by spring directly proportional displacement
![[8 Physics/Images/hookes law.png|400]]
---
### hookes formula

$$
\begin{aligned}
F_{el} = -k x \\
k = \text{spring constant} \\
x = \text{position}
\end{aligned}
$$

---
### hookes work
- transfer of energy from the application of restoring force causing displacement parallel force
![[8 Physics/Images/hookes work.png|300]]
---
### hookes work formula

$$
\begin{aligned}
W = k\int_{x_1}^{x_2} \overrightarrow x \cdot d \overrightarrow x = \frac{1}{2}kx^2 \\
k = \text{spring constant} \\
x = \text{position}
\end{aligned}
$$

---
### kinetic energy
- energy of motion
---
### kinetic energy formula

$$
\begin{aligned}
K = \frac{1}{2}mv^2 \\
m = \text{mass} \\
v = \text{velocity}
\end{aligned}
$$

---
### net work
- work done by net force acting on object
![[8 Physics/Images/net work.png]]
---
### net work formula

$$
\begin{aligned}
W_{net} = \overrightarrow F_{net} \cdot d \overrightarrow r = m\frac{d \overrightarrow v}{dt} \cdot d \overrightarrow r = m\overrightarrow v \cdot d \overrightarrow v \\
W_{net} = m\int_{v_1}^{v_2} \overrightarrow v \cdot d \overrightarrow v = \frac{1}{2}m v^2 \\
F = \text{force} \\
r = \text{position} \\
m = \text{mass} \\
v = \text{velocity} \\
t = \text{time}
\end{aligned}
$$

---
### work energy
- work done by net force equal change of kinetic energy
---
### work energy formula

$$
\begin{aligned}
W_{net} = K_f - K_i \\
K = \text{kinetic energy}
\end{aligned}
$$

---
### average power
- rate of energy transfer
---
### average power formula

$$
\begin{aligned}
P_{avg} = \frac{E}{t} \\
E = \text{energy} \\
t = \text{time}
\end{aligned}
$$

---
### instantaneous power
- rate of doing work
---
### instantaneous power formula

$$
\begin{aligned}
P = \overrightarrow F \cdot \overrightarrow v \\
F = \text{force} \\
v = \text{velocity}
\end{aligned}
$$

---
### efficiency
- percentage of power input conversion power output
---
### efficiency formula

$$
\begin{aligned}
\varepsilon = \frac{P_{out}}{P_{in}} \times 100 \\
P_{out} = \text{output power} \\
P_{in} = \text{input power}
\end{aligned}
$$
