proof

- valid argument that establish the truth of statement
---
argument

- sequence of premises that end with conclusion
---
premise

- all propositions except the final proposition
---
conclusion

- the final proposition
---
argument validity

- valid argument if and only if all premises true and conclusion true
- prove argument validity by proving valid argument form
---
argument form

- sequence of compound propositions involving propositional variables
---
argument formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\because \text{premise(s)} \\
\therefore \text{conclusion}
\end{align*}
$$
---
propositional rule of inference

- valid argument form prove argument validity
![[Discrete Mathematics/Images/propositional rule of inference.png]]
---
modus ponens

- hypothesis and conditional prove conclusion
---
modus ponens formula

$$
\begin{align*}
p \\
p \to q \\
\therefore q 
\end{align*}
$$
---
fallacy

- invalid argument based on contingency rather than tautology
---
fallacy of affirming the conclusion

- argument false when p false and q true
---
formula of affirming the conclusion

$$
\begin{align*}
q \\
p \to q \\
\therefore p
\end{align*}
$$
---
fallacy of denying the hypothesis

- argument false when p false and q true
---
formula of denying the hypothesis

$$
\begin{align*}
\neg p \\
p \to q \\
\therefore \neg q
\end{align*}
$$
---
instantiation

- general prove instance
---
generalization

- instance prove general
---
predicate rule of inference

- valid argument form prove argument validity
![[Discrete Mathematics/Images/predicate rule of inference.png]]
---
universal modus tollens

- proposition instance and general conditional prove conclusion instance
---
universal modus tollens formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\neg Q(a) \\
\forall x(P(x) \to Q(x) \\
\therefore \neg P(a)
\end{align*}
$$
