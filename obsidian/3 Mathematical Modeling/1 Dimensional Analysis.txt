mathematical model

- equation that express relationship between given quantity(s) of interest
---
unit

- scale of measurement
---
unit example

- meter
- second
- kilogram
- kelvin
---
dimension

- type of measurement
---
dimension formula

$$
\begin{align*}
[q] = \prod_{i=1}^m D_i^{a_i} \rightarrow \Delta_q = [a_1, \dots, a_m] \in \mathbb R^m \\
q = \text{quantity} \\ 
a = \text{dimensional exponent}
\end{align*}
$$
---
dimension example

- length
- time
- mass
- temperature
---
dimension example formula

- $q = 3 \frac{kg \cdot m^2}{s^2}$ 
- $D = \set{L, T, M, \theta}$ 
- $[q] = ML^2T^{-2}$ 
- $\Delta_q = [2, -2, 1, 0]$ 
---
dimensional basis

- spanning
- linearly independent
---
dimensional basis formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\text{Span}(D) = \mathcal V \\
\text{Rank}(D) = m
\end{align*}
$$
---
dimension property

- addition
- multiplication
- division
- exponentiation
- integration
- differentiation
---
dimension property formula

$$
\begin{align*}
[p \pm q] \in D \leftrightarrow [p] = [q] \\
[p \cdot q] = [p] \cdot [q] \\
[\frac{p}{q}] = \frac{[p]}{[q]} \\
[q^k] = [q]^k \\
[\int p \cdot dq] = [p] \cdot [q] \\
[\frac{d^kp}{dq^k}] = \frac{[p]}{[q]^k}
\end{align*}
$$
---
exponent property

- multiplication
- division
- exponentiation
---
exponent property formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\Delta_{pq} = \Delta_p + \Delta_q \\
\Delta_{p/q} = \Delta_p - \Delta_q \\
\Delta_{q^k} = k\Delta_q
\end{align*}
$$
---
dimensionless

- dimension equal 1
- dimensional exponent equal 0
---
dimensionless formula

$$
\begin{align*}
[q] = 1 \rightarrow \Delta_q = 0 \\
q = \text{pure number}
\end{align*}
$$
---
change of units

- convert unit of quantity with respect to dimensional basis
---
change of units formula

$$
\begin{align*}
q' = q\prod_{i=1}^m \lambda_i^{a_i} \\
q = \text{quantity} \\
\lambda = \text{unit conversion factor} \\
a = \text{dimensional exponent}
\end{align*}
$$
---
change of units example

- $q = 9.8 \frac{m}{s^2}$ 
- $U = \set{\text{m}, \text{s}}$ 
- $U' = \set{\text{km}, \text{min}}$ 
---
change of units example formula

$$
\begin{align*}
q' = (9.8\frac{\text{m}}{\text{s}^2})(\frac{1}{1000}\frac{\text{km}}{\text{m}})(\frac{60}{1}\frac{\text{s}}{\text{min}})^{2} = 35.28 \frac{\text{km}}{\text{min}^2}
\end{align*}
$$
---
unit-free equation

- every quantity of equation equal dimensionless quantity
---
unit-free equation formula

$$
\begin{align*}
q_1 = f(q_2, \dots, q_n) \rightarrow q_1' = f'(q_2', \dots, q_n')
\end{align*}
$$
---
unit-free example

- for every dimension assign arbitrary unit conversion factor
- change of units in terms of arbitrary unit conversion factor
- cancel all unit conversion factors such that sum of dimensional exponent equal zero
---
unit-free example formula

- $x = \frac{1}{2}gt^2$ 
- $D = \set{L, T}$ 
- $[x] = L, [t] = T, [g] = LT^{-2}$ 
- $x' = x\lambda_1, t' = t\lambda_2, g' = g\lambda_1\lambda_2^{-2}$ 
- $(x'\lambda_1^{-1}) = \frac{1}{2}(g'\lambda_1^{-1}\lambda_2^2)(t'\lambda_2^{-1})^2$ 
- $x'\lambda_1^{-1}\lambda_1^{1} = \frac{1}{2}g't'^2\lambda_2^2\lambda_2^{-2}$ 
- $\lambda \not\in x'$ 
---
power product

- product of power of quantity with respect to quantitative exponent
---
power product formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\pi = \prod_{i=1}^n q_i^{b_i} > 0 \\
q = \text{quantity} \\
b = \text{quantitative exponent}
\end{align*}
$$
---
full

- number of dimensionless power products equal the number of linearly independent solutions
---
full formula

$$
\begin{align*}
k = n - \text{Rank}(A) = \dim(\ker A) \\
n = \text{number of quantities} \\
A = \text{dimensional matrix} \\
\end{align*}
$$
---
normal

- explicit equation in terms of first quantity where 1st solution of 1st quantitative exponent equal 1 and remaining solution of 1st quantitative exponent equal zero
---
normal formula

$$
\begin{align*}
q_1 = f(q_2, \dots, q_n) \sim \pi_1 = \phi (\pi_2, \dots, \pi_k) \\
q = \text{quantity} \\
\pi = \text{dimensionless power product} 
\end{align*}
$$
---
buckingham pi property

- calculate unit
- form matrix whose columns equal units
- convert matrix into reduced row echelon form
- back substitute for the particular solution of system of linear equations
- full, normal, dimensionless power product equal the fundamental solution set
- every unit-free equation expressible as full, normal, dimensionless power product
---
buckingham pi property formula

$$
\begin{align*}
[\pi] = 1 \leftrightarrow \Delta_\pi = \sum_{i=1}^n b_i\Delta_{q_i} = A\vec v = 0 \\
A = [\Delta q_1, \dots, \Delta q_n] \in \mathcal M_{mn} \\
\vec v = [b_1, \dots, b_n] \in \mathbb R^n \\
\pi = \text{dimensionless power product} \\
b = \text{quantitative exponent}
\end{align*}
$$
