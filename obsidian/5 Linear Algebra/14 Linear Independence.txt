linear dependence

- zero vector expressible as nontrivial linear combination of the vectors of subset
---
linear dependence formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\vec x \ne \vec 0 \rightarrow \text{Rank}(S) < n \\
S = \{\vec v_1, \dots, \vec v_n\} \subseteq \mathcal V \\
S = \text{subset} \\
n = \text{dimension} \\
\vec x = \text{solution} \\
\mathcal V = \text{vector space}
\end{align*}
$$
---
linear independence

- zero vector not expressible as nontrivial linear combination of the vectors of subset
---
linear independence formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\vec x = \vec 0 \rightarrow \text{Rank}(S) = n \\
S = \{\vec v_1, \dots, \vec v_n\} \subseteq \mathcal V \\
S = \text{subset} \\
n = \text{dimension} \\
\vec x = \text{solution} \\
\mathcal V = \text{vector space}
\end{align*}
$$
---
empty set

- linearly independent set with zero elements
---
empty set formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\emptyset = \{\}
\end{align*}
$$
---
linear independence example

- standard unit vector
- standard unit polynomial
- standard unit matrix
---
linear independence example formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\vec e \\
\vec p \\
\psi_{ij}
\end{align*}
$$
---
single element linear independence

- subset with single nonzero vector equal linearly independent subset
---
single element linear independence formula

$$
\begin{align*}
(S = \{\vec v\}) \land (\vec v \ne 0) \rightarrow \text{Rank}(S) = n \\
S = \text{subset} \\
\vec v = \text{vector} \\
c = \text{scalar} \\
n = \text{dimension}
\end{align*}
$$
---
scalar multiplicity property

- subset without scalar multiple of vector equal linearly independent subset
---
scalar multiplicity property formula

$$
\begin{align*}
(\vec 0 = \sum_i^n c_i\vec v_i) \land (\not\exists \vec v_i = c\vec v_j) \rightarrow \text{Rank}(S) = n \\
S = \text{subset} \\
\vec v = \text{vector} \\
c = \text{scalar} \\
n = \text{dimension}
\end{align*}
$$
---
zero vector property

- subset with at least 1 zero vector equal linearly dependent subset
---
zero vector property formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\vec 0 \in S \rightarrow \text{Rank}(S) < n \\
S = \text{subset} \\
n = \text{dimension}
\end{align*}
$$
---
independence test

- generate matrix whose columns equal the vectors of subset
- form the reduced row echelon of the system
- if number of pivot columns equal number of columns then linearly independent subset
- if number of pivot columns not equal number of columns then linearly dependent subset
---
size property

- for collection of $n$ vectors, each with linear combination of the same $m$ coordinates, if $n = m$ then linear independence
- for collection of $n$ vectors, each with linear combination of the same $m$ coordinates, if $n > m$ then linear dependence
---
size property formula

$$
\begin{align*}
(S = \{\vec v_1 \dots, \vec v_n\}) \land (\vec v \in \mathbb R^m) \land (n = m) \rightarrow \text{Rank}(S) = n \\
(S = \{\vec v_1 \dots, \vec v_n\}) \land (\vec v \in \mathbb R^m) \land (n > m) \rightarrow \text{Rank}(S) \ne n
\end{align*}
$$
---
redundant vector

- vector expressible as linear combination of other vectors of subset
---
redundant vector formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\vec v \leftrightarrow \text{Span}(S) = \text{Span}(S - \{\vec v\})\\
\vec v = \text{redundant vector} \\
S = \text{subset}
\end{align*}
$$
---
redundancy property

- subset without redundant vector equal linearly independent subset
---
redundancy property formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\nexists \vec v \in S \rightarrow \text{Rank}(S) = n \\
\forall (2 \le k \le n): \vec v_k \not\in \text{Span}(\{\vec v_1 \dots \vec v_{k-1} \}) \\
\vec v = \text{redundant vector} \\
S = \text{subset} \\
n = \text{number of columns}
\end{align*}
$$
---
infinite linear independence

- every finite subset of infinite subset equal linearly independent subset
---
infinite linear independence formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\text{Rank}(S_1, \dots, S_k) = n \rightarrow \text{Rank}(S) = n \\
S_1 \dots S_n \subseteq S \subseteq \mathcal V \\
S = \text{infinite subset} \\
\mathcal V = \text{vector space} 
\end{align*}
$$
---
existence and uniqueness

- there exists unique linear combination of the vectors of subset for every element of the span of subset
---
existence and uniqueness formula

$$
\begin{align*}
\forall \vec v \in \text{Span}(S)\exists !(c_1 \vec v_1 + \dots c_k \vec v_k) \rightarrow \text{Rank}(S) = n \\
\vec v = \text{vector} \\
S = \text{subset} \\
c = \text{scalar} \\
n = \text{dimension}
\end{align*}
$$
---
summary

- zero coefficient
- no redundant vector
- finite subset linear independence
